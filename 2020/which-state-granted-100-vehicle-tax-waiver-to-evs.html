Which state granted 100% vehicle tax waiver to EVs?

(A) Kerala

(B) Karnataka

(C) ** Tamil Nadu

(D) Assam

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In a much-awaited move, the Tamil Nadu government has issued an order notifying the extension of a 100 per cent motor vehicle tax exemption for electric vehicles to be registered in the State. The exemption will be offered to the battery-operated vehicles registered between January 1, 2023, and December 31, 2025.

Concept note-2: -Notes: The state government of Tamil Nadu has notified 100 per cent motor vehicle tax exemption for transport and non-transport Electric Vehicles. The state has also planned to set up the first exclusive park for the electric vehicle, for the first time in the country.

Concept note-3: -Karnataka government may end 100% road tax exemption for EVs.

Concept note-4: -Maharashtra. Maharashtra announced its EV Policy in 2021 intended to make the state the top EV producer by 2025. For electric 2-wheelers, the first 100, 000 units to be sold would qualify for subsidies – an incentive of ₹5, 000 per kWh of battery capacity – a maximum of ₹10, 000.
