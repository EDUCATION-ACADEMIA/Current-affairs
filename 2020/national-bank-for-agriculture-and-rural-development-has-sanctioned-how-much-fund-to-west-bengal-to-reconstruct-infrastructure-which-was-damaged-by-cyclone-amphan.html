National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development has sanctioned how much fund to West Bengal to reconstruct infrastructure which was damaged by Cyclone Amphan?

(A) Rs 595 crore

(B) ** Rs 795 crore

(C) Rs 695 crore

(D) Rs 895 crore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development has sanctioned Rs. 795 crore to the Government of West Bengal to reconstruct infrastructure damaged by Cyclone Amphan. This fund is approved under the Rural Infrastructure Development Fund.

Concept note-2: -The damages caused by the Amphan Cyclone were estimated at USD 13 billion. This cyclone was the costliest and deadliest to hit the Bay of Bengal Delta since 2007 and was a rare super cyclonic formation over the Bay of Bengal. We have shared more information about Cyclone Amphan here.

Concept note-3: -Amphan took 40 hours after its formation to become a super cyclone. The main reason behind this was the high sea surface temperatures of 32-34 degrees celsius in the Bay of Bengal. General long-term warming of the Bay of Bengal was the leading cause of rapid intensification, according to experts.

Concept note-4: -It killed around 100 people in the state alone. “Cyclone Amphan was the largest source of displacement in 2020, with 2.4 million displacements in India alone, out of which around 800, 000 was pre-emptive evacuation by the authorities, ” the report says.

Concept note-5: -National Bank for Agriculture and RuralDevelopment (NABARD) was established on 12 July 1982 by an Act of theParliament.
