Where did defence Indian coast guard vessel Kanaklata Barua commissioned?

(A) New Delhi

(B) Chennai

(C) ** Kolkata

(D) Mumbai

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Indian Coast Guard (ICG) on Wednesday commissioned ICGS Kanaklata Barua, a fast patrol vessel (FPV), in Kolkata. ICG ships are named after women freedom fighters of the country and the ICG had an FPV named after Kanaklata, which was decommissioned in 2017 after 20 years of service from Port Blair as its base.

Concept note-2: -“Rebasing of ICGS Arnvesh in Coast Guard district headquarter 4 will add to the power and operational capability of the force along the vast coastline of Kerala.

Concept note-3: -Indian Coast Guard ship ‘Vajra’, the sixth offshore patrol vessel to enhance coastal security, was formally commissioned into service in Chennai on 24 March 2021.

Concept note-4: -Indian Coast Guard’s fifth offshore patrol vessel Saksham commissioned in Goa.

Concept note-5: -The Indian Coast Guard has commissioned the fifth warship in the series of 105-meter long offshore patrol vessels (OPVs) ICGS Saksham at Goa Shipyard.
