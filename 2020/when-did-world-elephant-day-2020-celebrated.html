When did World Elephant Day 2020 celebrated?

(A) 7 August

(B) 9 August

(C) 10 August

(D) ** 12 August

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On August 12, 2012, the inaugural World Elephant Day was launched to bring attention to the urgent plight of Asian and African elephants. The elephant is loved, revered and respected by people and cultures around the world, yet we balance on the brink of seeing the last of this magnificent creature.

Concept note-2: -Every year on August 12, World Elephant Day is observed with an aim to acknowledge Elephantsâ€™ significance in our ecosystem. The day underlines the awareness of the threats that elephants face in their daily lives.

Concept note-3: -Why a World Elephant Day? World Elephant Day has been created to bring the world together to help elephants. It is a day to honor elephants, to spread awareness about the critical threats they are facing, and to support positive solutions that will help ensure their survival.

Concept note-4: -Elephant populations With only 40, 000-50, 000 left in the wild, the species is classified as endangered. And it is critical to conserve both African and Asian elephants since they play such a vital role in their ecosystems as well as contributing towards tourism and community incomes in many areas.
