At which place Country’s highest Meteorological Centre is located?

(A) Kargil

(B) Tso Moriri

(C) ** Leh

(D) Zanskar Valley

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -India’s highest Meteorological Centre is situated in Ladakh. It is located at 3, 500 metres above sea level in Leh. Dr Harsh Vardhan, Minister of Earth Sciences (MoES), virtually inaugurated the centre. The Leh centre is the second Meteorological Centre in the Himalayas, after Itanagar in Arunachal Pradesh.

Concept note-2: -The world’s highest meteorological station has been established on Mount Everest to study various weather phenomena. According to Nepal’s Department of Water and Meteorology, this meteorological station has been established at 8, 830 metres just below the summit point of the mountain range (maximum height 8848.86 m).

Concept note-3: -India’s highest meteorological centre has been inaugurated in Ladakh.

Concept note-4: -The Director General of Meteorology is the Head of the India Meteorological Department, with headquarters at New Delhi.

Concept note-5: -In the year 1875, the Government of India established the India Meteorological Department, bringing all meteorological work in the country under a central authority.
