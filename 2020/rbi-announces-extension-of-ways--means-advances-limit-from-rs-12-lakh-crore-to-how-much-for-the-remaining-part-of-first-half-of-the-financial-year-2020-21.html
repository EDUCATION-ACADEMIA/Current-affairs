RBI announces extension of Ways & Means Advances limit from Rs 1.2 lakh crore to how much for the remaining part of first half of the financial year 2020-21?

(A) Rs 1.5 lakh crore

(B) Rs 1.8 lakh crore

(C) Rs 2.5 lakh crore

(D) ** Rs 2 lakh crore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It has been decided, in consultation with the Government of India, that the limit for Ways and Means Advances (WMA) for the second half of the financial year 2022-23 (October 2022 to March 2023) will be ₹50, 000 crore.

Concept note-2: -Considering the uncertainties related to COVID-19, the RBI had raised the WMA limit to ₹51, 560 crores for all States. The higher WMA was applicable till March 31, 2022. How the central government meets the temporary cash needs?

Concept note-3: -To take care of temporary mismatches in Government accounts, the Reserve Bank of India has fixed the Ways and Mean Advances (WMA) limit for H2 of FY 2022-23 at ₹50, 000 crore.

Concept note-4: -As announced in the Monetary Policy Statement 2022-23 dated December 07, 2022, the Bank Rate is revised upwards by 35 basis points from 6.15 per cent to 6.50 per cent with immediate effect.
