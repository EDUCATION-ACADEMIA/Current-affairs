India’s foreign exchange reserves have surged by how much dollars to cross the half-a-trillion mark for the first time?

(A) 6.22 billion dollars

(B) 5.22 billion dollars

(C) 7.22 billion dollars

(D) ** 8.22 billion dollars

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The total forex reserves touched an all time high of 642.453 billion US$ on 8 September 2021.

Concept note-2: -According to RBI’s latest data, India’s foreign currency assets, the biggest component of the forex reserves, rose by USD 9.078 billion to USD 505.519 billion. Gold reserves rose by USD 1.106 to USD 42.890 billion. Notably, at the start of last year - 2022, the overall forex reserves were at about USD 633 billion.

Concept note-3: -Reserves rose to $572 billion, the highest since early August last year. The number is higher than the earlier week - ended January 6 - at $561.58 billion. India’s reserves hit a more-than-two-year low of $524.52 billion in October 2022.

Concept note-4: -India’s foreign exchange reserves fell by $70.1 billion in 2022, data released by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) shows. The forex reserves stood at $562.9 billion in the week ended December 30.
