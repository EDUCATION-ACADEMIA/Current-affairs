Ebrahim Alkazi who passed away recently is associated with which of the following department?

(A) Sports

(B) ** Film industry

(C) Author

(D) Scientist

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Alkazi, who was the longest serving director of the National School of Drama and mentored generations of actors, produced plays such as Girish Karnad’s “Tughlaq” and Dharamvir Bharati’s “Andha Yug"

Concept note-2: -As the director of National School of Drama (1962–77), Alkazi devised the vision, system and pedagogy of theatre in India. He revitalised not only NSD, but revolutionalised/channelised the destiny of Indian theatre. He was one of the nation builders instrumental in the cultural and emotional integration of the country.

Concept note-3: -Bharata Muni, the founding father of Indian drama, is known for his crucial treatise on theatre, the Natya Shastra, which describes two main genres of plays: Natakas and Prakarana.

Concept note-4: -In 1962, Alkazi embarked upon his journey to develop a pedagogic discourse for theatre in form of the National School of Drama.

Concept note-5: -The Sangeet Natak Akademi appointed Ebrahim Alkazi, 37, to head the newly conceived National School of Drama (NSD) in 1962. The multifaceted and charismatic Alkazi was an established name in theatre.
