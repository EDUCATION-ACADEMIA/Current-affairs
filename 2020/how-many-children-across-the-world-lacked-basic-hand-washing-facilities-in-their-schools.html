How many children across the world lacked basic hand-washing facilities in their schools?

(A) Around 718 Million

(B) ** Around 818 Million

(C) Around 858 Million

(D) Around 1000 Million

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -At the current rates of progress, 1.6 billion people will lack safely managed drinking water, 2.8 billion people will lack safely managed sanitation, and 1.9 billion people will lack basic hand hygiene facilities in 2030.

Concept note-2: -When they eat without cleaning their hands, the germs on their fingers get transferred to the inside of their mouth. It can cause severe illnesses, such as diarrhoea, laryngitis, coughs, colds, and stomach infections. Hence, washing hands thoroughly must be made mandatory for children before and after eating.

Concept note-3: -Find information on how safe water, sanitation, and hygiene service levels are defined. 2 billion people lack access to safely managed drinking water at home. Of those, 1.2 billion people have basic drinking water service.

Concept note-4: -Problem statement: Hand washing with soap is an important means of preventing hospital acquired infections.
