When did the World Animal day being celebrated?

(A) 1 October

(B) 2 October

(C) 3 October

(D) ** 4 October

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -World Animal Day is an annual event on the 4th of October, celebrating animal rights and welfare across the globe. It’s an important day and has been marked since 1925, with a growing international community striving to end the needless suffering of all animals.

Concept note-2: -World Animal Day was first celebrated on 24th March 1925 in Germany. It was started by Heinrich Zimmermann, a cynologist, who organised the first celebration at the Sports Palace in Berlin, which was reportedly attended by over 5, 000 people.

Concept note-3: -The day is also known as ‘Animal Lovers Day’ because it encourages love, care, affection, and protection of animals by supporting individuals and organisations who promote animal rights. To make the world a better place for all animals, the day rallies the animal welfare movement into a global force.

Concept note-4: -World Animal Day is celebrated every year on October 4 to raise awareness about improving the welfare standards of animals across the globe.

Concept note-5: -The first celebration of World Animal Day was observed in March, 1925.
