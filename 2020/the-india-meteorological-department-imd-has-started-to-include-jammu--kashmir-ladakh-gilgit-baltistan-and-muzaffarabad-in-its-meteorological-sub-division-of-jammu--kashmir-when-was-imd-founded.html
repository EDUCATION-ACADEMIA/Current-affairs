The India Meteorological Department (IMD) has started to include Jammu & Kashmir, Ladakh, Gilgit-Baltistan, and Muzaffarabad in its meteorological sub-division of Jammu & Kashmir. When was IMD founded?

(A) 1775

(B) 1975

(C) ** 1875

(D) 1675

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Thus there are 6 sub-divisions named ‘East Rajasthan’, ‘West Rajasthan’, ‘East Uttar Pradesh’, ‘West Uttar Pradesh’, ‘East Madhya Pradesh’ and ‘West Madhya Pradesh’.

Concept note-2: -In the year 1875, the Government of India established the India Meteorological Department, bringing all meteorological work in the country under a central authority. Mr. H. F. Blanford was appointed Meteorological Reporter to the Government of India.

Concept note-3: -Organisation Structure in IMD For the convenience of administrative and technical control, there are 6 Regional Meteorological Centres, each under a Deputy Director General with headquarters at Mumbai, Chennai, New Delhi, Calcutta, Nagpur and Guwahati.

Concept note-4: -The India Meteorological Department (IMD) is the principal department responsible for meteorological observations and weather forecasting under Ministry of Earth Sciences, Government of India .
