Indian Railways operationalised its 1st 12, 000 horsepower electric made in India Locomotive, WAG12 from which station?

(A) ** Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Station

(B) Kanpur Central Station

(C) Prayagraj Chheoki Junction

(D) Lucknow Charbagh Station

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -First 12000 HP made in India Locomotive, manufactured by Madhepura Electric Loco Factory situated in Bihar, was put into operation by Indian Railways from Pt Deen Dayal Upadhyaya Jn Station yesterday. The loco is named WAG12 with Number 60027.

Concept note-2: -A total of 280 WAG-12B have been built at Electric Locomotive Factory, Madhepura, Bihar, India. With a power output of 12, 000 hp, the WAG 12 is twice as powerful as its immediate predecessor, WAG 9, and is among the most powerful freight locomotives in the world.

Concept note-3: -The first electric train ran in India with the inauguration of services between Bombay VT and Kurla Harbour on 3rd Feb 1925 on ex-GIP Railway system. The section was electrified on 1500 Volt DC.

Concept note-4: -The loco is named WAG 12 B with number 60100. The locomotive is manufactured by Madhepura Electric Locomotive Pvt. Ltd. (MELPL).

Concept note-5: -The engine was named WAG12 with Number 60027. It was a proud moment for Indian Railways, as it became the 6th country in the world to join the elite club of producing high horsepower locomotive indigenously. These locomotives are state of the art IGBT based, 3 phase drive and 12000 horsepower electric locomotive.
