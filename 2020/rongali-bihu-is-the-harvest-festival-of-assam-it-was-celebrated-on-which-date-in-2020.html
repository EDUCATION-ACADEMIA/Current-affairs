Rongali Bihu is the Harvest Festival of Assam. It was celebrated on which date in 2020?

(A) 13 April

(B) 12 April

(C) ** 14 April

(D) 11 April

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bohag Bihu (mid-April, also called Rongali Bihu), the most popular Bihu celebrates the onset of the Assamese New Year (around 14–15 April) and the coming of Spring.

Concept note-2: -Rongali Bihu, also known as Bohag (since it is held in the Assamese month of Bohag or Baisakh), is observed in mid-April (14-15th April) and marks the start of the Hindu calendar’s Assamese New Year.

Concept note-3: -People in Assam celebrated the Rongali Bihu festival with folk dances and the playing of their traditional drums. The festival marks the beginning of the Assamese calendar year and the onset of spring.

Concept note-4: -Kati Bihu 2022: Kongali or Kati Bihu is an auspicious festival which is celebrated in Assam with joy and happiness and it is being observed today on 18th October, 2022.

Concept note-5: -Day 1 (14th April 2023): Goru Bihu - Day for Household Cattle.
