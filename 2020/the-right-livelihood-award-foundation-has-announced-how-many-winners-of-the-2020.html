The Right livelihood Award Foundation has announced how many winners of the 2020?

(A) Two

(B) Three

(C) ** Four

(D) Five

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It honoured four distinct individuals for their contribution in the four formerly mentioned fields. These four laureates include Nasrin Sotoudeh from Iran, Bryan Stevenson from the United States, Lottie Cunningham Wren from Nicaragua and Ales Bialiatski from Belarus.

Concept note-2: -Somali-born Fartuun Adan and her daughter Ilwad Elman are in Stockholm to receive the 2022 Right Livelihood Award. They have been honored for their fight for human rights despite facing dangers.

Concept note-3: -The Right Livelihood Award 2022 is now open for nominations with a deadline of 2 March. The Award recognises the actions of brave visionaries and builds impactful connections around the world, boosting urgent and long-term social change.

Concept note-4: -It has become widely known as the ‘Alternative Nobel Prize’ and there are now 137 Laureates from 58 countries. Presented annually in Stockholm at a ceremony in the Swedish Parliament, the Right Livelihood Award is usually shared by four recipients although not all Laureates receive a cash award.
