Scientists Identified how many New Species of Butterflies in Matheran Near Mumbai?

(A) 70

(B) 90

(C) 27

(D) ** 77

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Winged beauties: The research paper provides a glimpse of the rare butterflies in the hill station. After a long gap of 125 years, scientists have found 140 rare species of butterflies, including 77 new ones, in Matheran.

Concept note-2: -The new species of butterfly, now named the Chocolate-bordered Flitter, also carries the scientific name Zographetus dzonguensis, after Dzongu in north Sikkim, the place where it was discovered.
