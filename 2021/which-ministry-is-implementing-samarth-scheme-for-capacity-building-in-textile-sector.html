Which ministry is implementing Samarth Scheme for Capacity Building in Textile Sector?

(A) ** Ministry of Textile

(B) Ministry of Home Affairs

(C) Ministry of Women and Child Development

(D) Ministry of Finance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The office of the Development Commissioner (Handicrafts) is implementing the SAMARTH to provide skill training to handicraft artisans under the component ‘Skill Development in Handicrafts Sector’ of National Handicrafts Development Programme (NHDP).

Concept note-2: -Shri Narayan Rane launches Special Entrepreneurship Promotion Drive for Women – “SAMARTH” on the occasion of International Women’s Day 2022.

Concept note-3: -The scheme is implemented through the following agencies: Textile Industries. Organisations of the Ministry of Textiles/State Governments possessing the entire training infrastructure, including placement linkages with the textile industry.

Concept note-4: -SAMARTH is a placement-oriented programme targeting skill development of 10 lakh youth in the entire value chain of textiles, excluding Spinning & Weaving in the organized sector. The Government launched the Scheme for Capacity Building in Textile Sectorii (SCBTS) and named it Samarth Scheme.

Concept note-5: -Ministry of Textiles has launched many schemes over the past few years for the promotion of the Textile Industry in India and improving the livelihood of the people associated with it. Schemes like Project SURE, Scheme For Integrated Textile Park (SITP), Silk Samagra, Powertex India, and Samarth were launched.
