The NSG conducted the counter-terrorism exercise in Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat and the national capital region. What is the name given to the annual drill?

(A) ** Gandiv

(B) Pinaka

(C) Shakti

(D) Vijaya

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The third edition of the week-long annual exercise called ‘Gandiv’ was launched at these locations by the NSG from August 22 and it will continue till August 28, an official statement issued on August 23 said. Gandiv was the name of Arjuna’s bow in Mahabharata.

Concept note-2: -The NSG is divided into four groups -Special Action Group, which looks after counterterrorist and counterinsurgency operations and consists of commandos and officers from the Indian Army and CAPFs. Then there is the Special Ranger Group, which is a counterterror force and is used along with the SAG.

Concept note-3: -There are 2 Special Action Groups – 51 and 52 SAGs. Both, along with 11 SRG, constitute the counter terrorist force (CTF) which is the counter-terrorist arm of NSG. 51 SAG along with 11 SRG are tasked for counter-terrorist and counter insurgency operations while 52 SAG deal with anti-hijacking operations.

Concept note-4: -Candidates are a part of the Indian Armed Forces which can be accomplished either by UPSC’s CDS and CAPF examination or any other state selection procedure. The minimum number of service years required in the Indian Army is 3 years but in the case of the police force candidates must have a 5-year service experience.
