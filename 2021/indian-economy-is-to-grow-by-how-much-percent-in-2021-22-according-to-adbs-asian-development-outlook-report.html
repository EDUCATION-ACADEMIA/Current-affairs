Indian economy is to grow by how much percent in 2021-22 according to ADB’s Asian Development Outlook report?

(A) 6%

(B) 7%

(C) 8%

(D) ** 5%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -ADB’s 7 per cent growth projection for fiscal 2022-23 (April 2022 to March 2023), unchanged from its September forecast, compares to 8.7 per cent GDP growth in 2021-22.

Concept note-2: -Full Year GDP Growth in India averaged 6.12 percent from 2006 until 2023, reaching an all time high of 8.70 percent in 2022 and a record low of -6.60 percent in 2021. This page includes a chart with historical data for India Full Year GDP Growth.

Concept note-3: -Developing Asia Outlook East Asia’s growth forecasts are revised down to 2.9% in 2022-due to the mobility restrictions in the People’s Republic of China plus deep contraction in Hong Kong, China-and down to 4.0% in 2023.

Concept note-4: -India GDP Growth Slows to 6.3% in Q3 Private spending slowed sharply (9.7% vs 25.9%), investment growth halved (10.4% vs 20.1%), and both exports (11.5% vs 14.7%) and imports (25.4% vs 37.2%) rose at a slower pace. Meanwhile, stocks declined faster (-19.4% vs -17.4%) and public expenditure shrank (-4.4% vs 1.3%).

Concept note-5: -Following a contraction of 7.3% in 2020-21, the Indian economy is expected to grow by 9.2% in real terms in 2021-22 (according to initial advanced projections). GDP is expected to grow in real terms by 8-8.5% in 2022-23.
