The UNESCO World Heritage Site of Hampi is in which state?

(A) Kerala

(B) ** Karnataka

(C) Gujarat

(D) Madhya Pradesh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The austere and grandiose site of Hampi comprise mainly the remnants of the Capital City of Vijayanagara Empire (14th-16th Cent CE), the last great Hindu Kingdom. The property encompasses an area of 4187, 24 hectares, located in the Tungabhadra basin in Central Karnataka, Bellary District.

Concept note-2: -In 1986, Hampi was declared a World Heritage site by UNESCO.

Concept note-3: -Karnataka has two UNESCO World Heritage Sites that are popular destinations and major tourist attractions- Hampi and Pattakadal. Hampi boasts of the ruins from the Vijayanagar Empire and is an ancient village located in North Karnataka, along the banks of the river Tungabhadra.

Concept note-4: -A UNESCO World Heritage site, the ancient village of Hampi in the south Indian state of Karnataka once served as the capital of one of the largest kingdoms of India, the Vijayanagara Empire.
