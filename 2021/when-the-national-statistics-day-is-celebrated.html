When the National Statistics Day is celebrated?

(A) 26 June

(B) 27 June

(C) 28 June

(D) ** 29 June

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In recognition to the notable contributions made by (late) Professor Prasanta Chandra Mahalanobis in the fields of statistics and economic planning, the Government of India has been celebrating his birth Anniversary, on 29th June, as “Statistics Day”.

Concept note-2: -The National Statistics Day of India is observed on the 29th of June every year by arranging seminars, programmes, and campaigns across the country. The first National Statistics Day was commemorated in 2007. Following that event, a proclamation was released in the Indian Gazette on June 5, 2007.

Concept note-3: -The theme of this year is ‘Data for Sustainable Development’.

Concept note-4: -The next time the day was observed was in 2015, with the topic ‘Better data, better lives’. The same year, the United Nations General Assembly declared that World Statistics Day would thereafter be celebrated once every 5 years. It was a resolution that was agreed upon on 3 June 2015.

Concept note-5: -29 June: National Statistics Day The day is observed on 29 June to popularise the use of Statistics in everyday life. The day commemorates the birth anniversary of Prof. P C Mahalanobis.
