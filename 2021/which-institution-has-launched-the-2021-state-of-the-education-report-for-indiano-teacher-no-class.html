Which Institution has launched the 2021 State of the Education Report for India:No Teacher, No Class?

(A) ** UNESCO

(B) UNFCCC

(C) UNDP

(D) UNICEF

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -New Delhi, 5 October 2021: Today, on the occasion of World Teacher’s Day, the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) launched its 2021 State of the Education Report (SOER) for India: “No Teacher, No Class”.

Concept note-2: -Gender dimension: The literacy rate in India is 74.04 percent, 82.14 percent for males and 65.46 percent for females. Teaching issues: According to UNESCO (2021), high pupil-teacher ratios and lack of professionally qualified teachers are the biggest challenges in India’s education system, and need a lot of attention.

Concept note-3: -This publication is the annual flagship report of UNESCO New Delhi and it is based on extensive research. The substance of the Report has been developed by an expert team of researchers in the Tata Institute of Social Sciences, Mumbai, under the guidance of the UNESCO New Delhi Office. Read the Press Release.

Concept note-4: -UNESCO launches 2022 State of the Education Report for India: Artificial Intelligence in Education.
