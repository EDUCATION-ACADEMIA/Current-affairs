Muslim Women Rights Day is being celebrated all over the country on which date?

(A) July 23

(B) ** August 1

(C) July 30

(D) July 29

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Muslim Women’s Rights Day is observed every year on the 01st of August to celebrate the enforcement of the law against the ‘Triple Talaq’ rule among Muslims.

Concept note-2: -The practice of talaq-e-biddat is said to have been around since the period of Caliph Umar, more than 1400 years ago. The Supreme Court described it as “manifestly arbitrary” and said that it allows a man to “break down [a] marriage whimsically and capriciously". Instant divorce is termed talaq-e-bid’at.

Concept note-3: -The Quran explicitly states that men and women are equal in the eyes of God. Furthermore, the Quran: forbids female infanticide (practiced in pre-Islamic Arabia and other parts of the world) instructs Muslims to educate daughters as well as sons.

Concept note-4: -In Islam a woman is a completely independent personality. She can make any contract or bequest in her own name. She is entitled to inherit in her position as mother, as wife, as sister and as daughter. She has perfect liberty to choose her husband.
