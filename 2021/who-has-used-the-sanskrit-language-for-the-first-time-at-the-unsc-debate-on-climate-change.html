Who has used the Sanskrit language for the first time at the UNSC debate on climate change?

(A) Nitin Gadkari

(B) Amit Shah

(C) ** Prakash Javadekar

(D) J P Nadda

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Starting his UNSC debate on climate change, Javadekar addressed other dignitaries with the Sanskrit hymn from Sukla Yajurveda.

Concept note-2: -India even suggested that it would support a more limited draft that focused exclusively on the Sahel region of North Africa, where desertification of arid areas is directly sparking water-related conflict, but this was not considered, and India then recorded its first negative vote in this term at the UNSC.

Concept note-4: -The draft resolution sought to create a formal space in the Security Council for discussions on climate change and its implications on international security.
