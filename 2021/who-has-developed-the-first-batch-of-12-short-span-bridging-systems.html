Who has developed the first batch of 12 Short Span Bridging systems?

(A) Bharat Electronics Limited

(B) Larsen & Toubro

(C) ** Defence Research and Development Organisation

(D) Reliance Naval & Engineering Limited

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Army on Friday inducted the first production lot of 12 Short Span Bridging System (SSBS)-10m, designed and developed by the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in association with Larsen & Toubro Limited, the production agency.

Concept note-2: -Research & Development Establishment (Engrs) Pune, a premier engineering laboratory of DRDO, has designed and developed the system in association with M/s L&T Ltd.

Concept note-3: -The deployed bridge is of the load classification of MLC 70. The system will help in the quick movement of troops and enhance the mobilization of resources, DRDO statement said. Short Span Bridging System is a mechanically launched bridging system of MLC-70 load class to bridge the smaller gaps and canals.

Concept note-4: -A bridging system is a device that enables voice communication among disparate radio frequencies, systems, or technologies.

Concept note-5: -Indian Army achieved a remarkable feat by constructing a bridge in 6 days. The construction of the bailey bridge over Sukhtawa river in Narmadapuram, Madhya Pradesh was completed by the Indian Army Engineers of Sudarshan Chakra Corps on August 31.
