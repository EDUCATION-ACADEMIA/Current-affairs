As per the report of Goldman Sachs, what is the projected GDP growth rate of the Indian economy in the financial year 2021-22?

(A) 8.8%

(B) 10.5%

(C) ** 9.1%

(D) 7.2%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -India’s GDP grew by 8.7% in 2021-22. Further, the firm expects headline retail inflation to ease to 6.1% next year from an estimated 6.8% this year.

Concept note-2: -Real GDP growth is estimated at 7.0 per cent in 2022-23, according to the first advance estimate of the National Statistical Office (NSO).

Concept note-3: -India’s economic growth in 2023 is expected to slow to 5.9%, from 6.9% in 2022, Goldman Sachs said in its India 2023 outlook report. “Growth will likely be a tale of two halves, with a slower first half as the reopening boost fades, and monetary tightening weighs on domestic demand.

Concept note-4: -The growth in real GDP during 2022-23 is estimated at 7 per cent as compared to 8.7 per cent in 2021-22, it stated.

Concept note-5: -The first advance estimate for 2022-23 released by the National Statistical Office (NSO) on Friday estimated GDP growth at 7%, slower than the 8.7% expansion posted in 2021-22. The estimate is a shade higher than the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), which pegged it at 6.8%.
