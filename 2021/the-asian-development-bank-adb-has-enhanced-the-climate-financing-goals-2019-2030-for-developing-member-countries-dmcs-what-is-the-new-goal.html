The Asian Development Bank (ADB) has enhanced the climate financing goals 2019-2030 for developing member countries (DMCs). What is the new goal?

(A) $120 billion

(B) $150 billion

(C) ** $100 billion

(D) $80 billion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Asian Development Bank (ADB) has announced an increase in its climate financing goals 2019-2030 for developing member countries (DMCs) by $20 billion to $100 billion. Earlier in 2018, ADB had announced an $80 billion goal for climate financing for developing countries in Asia during 2019-2030.

Concept note-2: -From 31 members at its establishment in 1966, ADB has grown to encompass 68 members-of which 49 are from within Asia and the Pacific and 19 outside.

Concept note-4: -Niue became the 68th Member of the Asian Development Bank in March 2019.

Concept note-5: -The Asian Development Bank (ADB) envisions a prosperous, inclusive, resilient, and sustainable Asia and the Pacific, while sustaining its efforts to eradicate extreme poverty in the region.
