Indian Oil will construct India’s first Green Hydrogen Plant in which city?

(A) ** Mathura

(B) Panipat

(C) Gurugram

(D) Faridabad

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -India’s largest oil firm IOC will build the nation’s first ‘green hydrogen’ plant at its Mathura refinery, as it aims to prepare for a future catering to the growing demand for both oil and cleaner forms of energy.

Concept note-2: -Gujarat: First phase of Green Hydrogen plant commissioned at L&T’s Hazira complex | Cities News, The Indian Express.

Concept note-3: -NTPC Ltd commissions India’s first green hydrogen blending project. The green hydrogen blending has been started in the piped natural gas (PNG) network of NTPC Kawas township, Surat. The project is a joint effort of NTPC and Gujarat Gas Limited (GGL).

Concept note-4: -New Delhi: India’s first green hydrogen fuelling station, being set up at Leh in the Union Territory of Ladakh, is all set to be commissioned before May next year. The first-of-its-kind project is being set up by state-run NTPC.
