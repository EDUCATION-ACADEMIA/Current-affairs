Which organisation proposed four-layered bank norms for NBFCs?

(A) ** RBI

(B) Finance Ministry

(C) NABARD

(D) DBS Bank

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Recently, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has proposed a tighter regulatory framework for Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) by creating a four-tier structure with a progressive increase in intensity of regulation.

Concept note-2: -The framework categorises NBFCs in Base Layer (NBFC-BL), Middle Layer (NBFC-ML), Upper Layer (NBFC-UL) and Top Layer (NBFC-TL).

Concept note-3: -The Reserve Bank has been given the powers under the RBI Act 1934 to register, lay down policy, issue directions, inspect, regulate, supervise and exercise surveillance over NBFCs that meet the 50-50 criteria of principal business.

Concept note-4: -The Audit Committee constituted by an NBFC as required under Section 292-A of the Companies Act, 1956 shall also be the Audit Committee for the purposes of RBI Regulations this paragraph and shall have the same powers, functions, duties as laid in Companies Act, 1956.

Concept note-5: -The banking system in India is regulated by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), through the provisions of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949.
