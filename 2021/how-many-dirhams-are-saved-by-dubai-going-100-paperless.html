How many Dirhams are saved by Dubai going 100% Paperless?

(A) ** Around 3 billion Dirham

(B) Around 5 billion Dirham

(C) Around 4 billion Dirham

(D) Around 2 billion Dirham

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Dubai became the world’s first government to turn 100% paperless, the announcement was made by the United Arab Emirate (UAE) Crown Prince, Sheikh Hamdan bin Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum. It will save around 3 billion Dirham (USD 350 million) and 14-million-man hours.

Concept note-2: -The correct answer is Dubai. Dubai has become the first government in the world to turn 100 per cent paperless. This was announced by Emirate’s Crown Prince Sheikh Hamdan bin Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum. The emirate has achieved the objectives of the ‘Dubai Paperless Strategy, which was launched in 2018.

Concept note-3: -Dubai has become the first nation in the world to be 100 percent paperless: How is it significant? Dubai’s paperless strategy has five significant phases.

Concept note-4: -The Dubai Paperless Strategy aims to build a perfectly integrated paperless government framework, and an administration that sets solid plans and strategies to secure people’s happiness and develop their communities to meet the requirements of the cities of the future.

Concept note-5: -Collaboration and integration among participating entities enabled the automation of processes and services provided to customers, cutting paper consumption by more than 336 million papers, the statement added.
