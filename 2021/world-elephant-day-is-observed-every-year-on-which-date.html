World Elephant Day is observed every year on which date?

(A) August 10

(B) August 11

(C) ** August 12

(D) August 15

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -By India Today Web Desk: World Elephant Day is celebrated every year on August 12 across the world to spread the awareness of saving this precious animal. The elephant is loved, revered, and respected by people and cultures around the world.

Concept note-2: -On August 12, 2012, the inaugural World Elephant Day was launched to bring attention to the urgent plight of Asian and African elephants. The elephant is loved, revered and respected by people and cultures around the world, yet we balance on the brink of seeing the last of this magnificent creature.

Concept note-3: -World Elephant Day is celebrated to bring attention to the urgent plight of Asian and African Elephants. Act Now! “All tourist attractions that breed elephants for commercial purposes and promote direct contact with elephants, including rides, shows or tricks, should be avoided."

Concept note-4: -National Elephant Appreciation Day – September 22, 2023. U.S. National Elephant Appreciation Day is a great time to give these majestic and beautiful creatures the respect they deserve. Elephants are the largest land mammals on Earth, so it’s only right that they have a day completely dedicated to them.
