Which country has approved a three-child policy and has marked a major policy shift to prevent a steep decline in the birth rate in the country?

(A) ** China

(B) Japan

(C) Germany

(D) USA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The changes come in the wake of China’s once-in-ten year population census that recorded rapidly declining birth rates over the past decade.

Concept note-2: -Three-child policy (Chinese: ; pinyin: Sānhái Zhèngcè), whereby a couple can have three children, was a family planning policy in the People’s Republic of China.

Concept note-3: -The term one-child policy refers to a population planning initiative in China implemented between 1980 and 2015 to curb the country’s population growth by restricting many families to a single child.

Concept note-4: -According to the Decision of the Central Committee of the Chinese Communist Party and of the State Council by which the three-child policy was approved (dated 26 June 2021), the intention is to bring the sex ratio at birth back to a normal level by preventing the elimination of female foetuses.
