The Indian Army has issued RFI to procure how many Futuristic Infantry Combat Vehicles?

(A) ** 1750

(B) 2000

(C) 1400

(D) 1000

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In June 2021, the Indian Army issued a request for information (RFI) on the planned procurement of 1, 750 units of indigenously developed, amphibious, tracked FICVs.

Concept note-2: -Section: Section is the smallest of all the military outfits present in the Indian Army. A section consists of 10 military personnel.

Concept note-3: -Defence Futuristic Infantry Combat Vehicle (FICV) | Tata Motors Limited.

Concept note-4: -Tata Motors Xenon The Tata Xenon is a very versatile platform which is being put to multiple uses in the Indian army. The non armoured version of Tata Xenon is used as a troops carrier and the armoured version is a battle ready version of the Xenon with artillery mounted on its rear deck.

Concept note-5: -A, B, C class denotes the classification of the vehicle within the user group. Like only Tanks, Armoured personnel carriers and TATRA tank carriers will be A class vehicles. Most of the Gypsys and Jeeps would be B class while troop carriers and lorries would be D class and so on.
