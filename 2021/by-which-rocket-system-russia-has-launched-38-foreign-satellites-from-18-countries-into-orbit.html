By which rocket system Russia has launched 38 foreign satellites from 18 countries into orbit?

(A) Soyuz-2.1v

(B) Soyuz-2.1b

(C) ** Soyuz-2.1a

(D) Soyuz-2.1s

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -"The Soyuz-2.1a carrier rocket with the Fregat upper stage and 38 spacecraft from 18 countries took off from the Baikonur cosmodrome, ‚Äù Roscosmos said on its Twitter account. A Russian Soyuz rocket blasted off from the Baikonur cosmodrome in Kazakhstan.

Concept note-2: -The rocket will place in orbit 38 satellites from more than a dozen countries, including South Korea, Japan, Canada, Saudi Arabia, Germany, Italy and Brazil.

Concept note-3: -Artificial satellites can be either unmanned (robotic) or manned. The first artificial satellite to be placed in orbit was the unmanned Sputnik 1, launched October 4, 1957, by the Soviet Union. Since then, thousands have been sent into Earth orbit.

Concept note-4: -As of June 2022, USA designations have been assigned to 331 space satellites.
