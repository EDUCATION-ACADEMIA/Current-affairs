The 6th UN Global Road Safety Week has been organised in which week of May 2021?

(A) 15-21 May

(B) 11-17 May

(C) ** 17-23 May

(D) 13-19 May

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -“Streets for Life” is the slogan chosen for the 6th UN Global Traffic Safety Week, which this year is celebrated between 17 and 23 May, to call for 30 km/h (20 mph) speed limits to be the norm for cities, towns and villages worldwide.

Concept note-2: -Ministry of Road Transport & Highways, Government of India is observing the Road Safety Week from 11th to 17th January 2023, under “Swachhata Pakhwada", to propagate the cause of safer roads for all.

Concept note-3: -Every year, road crashes result in the loss of lakhs of lives and serious injuries to crores of people. The Ministry of Road Transport & Highways is observing the Road Safety Week from January 11th to 17th, 2023, under “Swachhata Pakhwada”, to propagate the cause of safer roads for all.

Concept note-4: -The theme of the 33rd Road Safety Week 2022 is ‘Sadak Suraksha Jeevan Raksha’. Road Safety Week is celebrated from January 11 to January 17 every year.

Concept note-5: -Road Safety Week is a campaign initiated by the Ministry of Road Transport & Highways. This year, we are celebrating 32nd Road Safety Week and its theme is “safe yourself to save your family”.
