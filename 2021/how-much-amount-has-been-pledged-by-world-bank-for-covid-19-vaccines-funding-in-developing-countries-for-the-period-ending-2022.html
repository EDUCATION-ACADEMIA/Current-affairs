How much amount has been pledged by World Bank for Covid-19 vaccines funding in developing countries for the period ending 2022?

(A) ** $20 billion

(B) $ 25 billion

(C) $12 billion

(D) $8 billion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As of June 30, 2022, the World Bank approved operations to support vaccine rollout in 78 countries amounting to $10.1 billion. Through this, the World Bank Group is working with partners on the largest vaccination effort in history to stop the COVID-19 pandemic.

Concept note-2: -The Wuhan Institute, part of the state-owned Chinese company Sinopharm, launched phase 3 trials for its vaccine in July 2020 in the UAE, and in Peru and Morocco in August 2020. On 25 February 2021, China announced the approval of the Wuhan vaccine for general use.

Concept note-3: -World Bank Approves $497 Million in Financing to Lower South Africa’s Greenhouse Gas Emissions and Support a Just Transition.

Concept note-4: -WASHINGTON, June 13, 2022 -Today, the World Bank Group Board of Executive Directors approved a 454.4 million euro (ZAR 7.6 billion or $480 million) loan for South Africa’s COVID-19 Emergency Response Project.
