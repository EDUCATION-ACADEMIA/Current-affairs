The FICCI has projected the GDP of India at which percent in FY21?

(A) -11%

(B) ** -8%

(C) -10%

(D) -6%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Industry body FICCI on Thursday said India’s economy is estimated to grow 7 per cent in the current fiscal, lower than the earlier projection of 7.4 per cent, mainly due to the ongoing geopolitical uncertainties.

Concept note-2: -Giving more details, the Minister stated that as per provisional estimates released by National Statistical Office (NSO), Indian economy in 2021-22 has fully recovered the pre-pandemic real GDP level of 2019-20. The real GDP growth in 2021-22 stands at 8.7 per cent, 1.5 per cent higher than the real GDP of 2019-20.

Concept note-3: -The latest round of FICCI’s Economic Outlook Survey puts forth an annual median GDP growth forecast for 2022-23 at 7.0 percent - with a minimum and maximum growth estimate of 6.5 percent and 7.3 percent respectively.

Concept note-4: -India’s GDP to grow 7.4% in 2022-23: FICCI survery | 5paisa Blog.

Concept note-5: -NSO First revised FY21 GDP estimates The National Statistical Office recently released the first revised GDP estimates for the fiscal year 2021. According to the estimates, the GDP contracted by 6.6%.
