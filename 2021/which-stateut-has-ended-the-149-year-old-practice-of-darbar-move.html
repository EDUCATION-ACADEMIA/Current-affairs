Which state/UT has ended the 149-year-old practice of ‘darbar move’?

(A) Madhya Pradesh

(B) Uttar Pradesh

(C) ** Jammu and Kashmir

(D) Ladakh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Q. Which state/UT recently ended 149-year-old practice of ‘darbar move’? Notes: The Government of Jammu & Kashmir recently ended 149-year-old practice of ‘darbar move’.

Concept note-2: -The practice of ‘Darbar Move’ included movement of hundreds of trucks to transport the official documents and infrastructure over a distance of over 300 km between Srinagar and Jammu and vice versa. The 149-year-old practice entailed an annual cost of around Rs 400 crore.

Concept note-3: -This practice is believed to be introduced in 1872 by Maharaja Ranbir Singh when Jammu & Kashmir was under Dogra rule. Singh governed J&K between 1856 and 1885 but the tradition endured after his rule came to an end with his death.

Concept note-4: -J&K’s 149-year-old ‘Darbar Move’ tradition ends, The process of moving the capital from one city to another twice a year, that is, the rule of “Darbar Move” has ended forever in Jammu and Kashmir. The government has stopped this custom which has been going on for 149 years.

Concept note-5: -The tradition was started by Maharajah Ghulab Singh in 1872.
