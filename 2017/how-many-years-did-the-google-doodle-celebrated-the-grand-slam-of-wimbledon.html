How many years did the Google Doodle celebrated the Grand Slam of Wimbledon?

(A) ** 140 years

(B) 135 years

(C) 125 years

(D) 128 years

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Today’s Doodle marks the 140th year of the Wimbledon championships, the world’s oldest tennis tournament.

Concept note-2: -Since the first tournament 125 years ago in 1877, The Championships have been hosted by the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club in Wimbledon, London and take place over two weeks in late June – early July.

Concept note-3: -Spencer Gore, a 27-year-old rackets player from Wandsworth, became the first Wimbledon champion by defeating William Marshall, a 28-year-old real tennis player, in three straight sets in a final that lasted 48 minutes. The tournament made a profit of £10.

Concept note-4: -The 2022 Wimbledon Championships was a Grand Slam tier tennis tournament that took place at the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club in Wimbledon, London, United Kingdom. Novak Djokovic successfully defended his gentlemen’s singles title to claim his 21st major title, defeating Nick Kyrgios in the final.

Concept note-5: -Wimbledon is the world’s oldest tennis tournament and is also widely considered to be the most prestigious. It has been held at the All England Club in Wimbledon, London since 1877. It is one of the four Grand Slam tennis tournaments (majors), the others being the Australian Open, the French Open and the US Open.
