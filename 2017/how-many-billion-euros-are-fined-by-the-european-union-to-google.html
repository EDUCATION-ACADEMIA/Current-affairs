How many billion Euros are fined by the European Union to Google?

(A) ** 2.42

(B) 3

(C) 5

(D) 1.58

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Google loses EU antitrust appeal, to pay €4.12 billion fine.

Concept note-2: -Google (GOOGL) suffered one of its biggest setbacks on Wednesday when a top European court fined it 4.125 billion euros ($4.13 billion) for using its Android mobile operating system to thwart rivals, offering a precedent for other regulators to ratchet up pressure.

Concept note-3: -Google Faces Record $4 Billion Fine In Europe After Losing Android Antitrust Appeal.

Concept note-4: -Here’s looking at why CCI has fined Google and the approach that the regulator wants the company to take. Essentially, the EU has fined Google a total of EUR 8.25 billion (approximately Rs 66, 600 crore) for antitrust violations after three investigations which spanned over a decade.

Concept note-5: -The €4.125bn (£3.5bn) penalty is the largest anti-trust fine ever handed down by the European Commission. It said Google had breached its laws by forcing Android phone-makers to carry its search and web browser apps in order to access the Google Play Store in 2018. Google has since changed its terms.
