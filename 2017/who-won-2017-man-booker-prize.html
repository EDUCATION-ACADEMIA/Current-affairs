Who won 2017 Man Booker Prize?

(A) The Sellout’ by Paul Beatty.

(B) All Nights

(C) Madrid the Day

(D) ** Lincoln in the Bardo”.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The 2017 Man Booker Prize was won by George Saunders for his polyphonic Lincoln in the the Bardo, proving perhaps that the dead can speak, eloquently. The short story writer’s first novel made him the second American to win the prize.

Concept note-2: -Man Booker Prize Shortlist Features George Saunders, Mohsin Hamid And Ali Smith. The 2017 Man Booker nominees are Paul Auster’s 4 3 2 1, Fiona Mozley’s Elmet, Ali Smith’s Autumn, Emily Fridlund’s History of Wolves, George Saunders’ Lincoln in the Bardo, and Mohsin Hamid’s Exit West.

Concept note-3: -The International Booker Prize is awarded to a book which is translated into English and published in the UK or Ireland. It is different from the Man Booker Prize, which is awarded to a work written in English, and has been won in the past by Indians.
