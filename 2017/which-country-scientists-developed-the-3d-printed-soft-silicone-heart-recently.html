Which country scientists developed the 3D-printed soft silicone Heart recently?

(A) ** Zurich

(B) Swedish

(C) Indian

(D) United States of America

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Israeli researchers have created an entire 3D-printed heart made from human cells in what they say is a world first.

Concept note-2: -Scientists in Israel on Monday unveiled a 3D print of a heart with human tissue and vessels. Israeli scientists on Monday unveiled what they said was the first-ever 3D printed heart made of real human tissues and vessels, calling it a first and a “major medical breakthrough” that advances possibilities for transplants.

Concept note-3: -In a major medical breakthrough, Tel Aviv University researchers have “printed” the world’s first 3D vascularised engineered heart using a patient’s own cells and biological materials. Their findings were published on April 15 in a study in Advanced Science.

Concept note-4: -JERUSALEM-Tel Aviv University scientists said on Monday that they had printed the first 3D heart, by using a patient’s cells and scans.

Concept note-5: -A team at the Tel Aviv University in Israel has achieved a major breakthrough by 3D-printing a heart with human tissue and vessels. In the future, this technology could be used to repair damaged hearts or to print entirely new ones, to be used for transplants.
