Which type of coaches are increased by railway department?

(A) Unreserved coach

(B) ** 3 AC coaches

(C) First Class

(D) Second class

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Indian Railways uses two coach types: one is ICF (Integral Coach Factory), and the other is LHB (Link Hofmann Bush).

Concept note-2: -General coaches number vary in a train, generally being 2 to 4 depending on the route. Whether the train is running on diesel or an electric track, it also depends on the route. Usually, the train has 2 just behind the engine and 2 at the end of the train, in general compartment coach.

Concept note-3: -Indian railways do add extra coaches sometimes when there is a high demand. The extra coach is identified as a suffix, e.g, AE1, CE1, SE1, SE2, etc. The extra coaches can be added under certain conditions only: The total number of coaches do not exceed the max length allowed.

Concept note-4: -First AC (1A) First AC sleeper Class of the Indian Railways are the most expensive mode of travelling. These are fully AC coaches. It consists of 4 or 2 berths per compartment and there are no side upper or side lower berths in 1A coaches.
