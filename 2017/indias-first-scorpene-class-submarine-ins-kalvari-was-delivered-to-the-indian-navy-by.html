India’s first Scorpene class submarine, INS Kalvari, was delivered to the Indian Navy by

(A) Bharat Forge

(B) Reliance Defence

(C) ** Mazagon Dock Shipbuilders Limited

(D) Larsen & Toubro

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -She was delivered to the Indian Navy on 21 September 2017 after successful completion of sea trials. She was commissioned by Prime Minister Narendra Modi on 14 December 2017 at Mazagon Dock Limited . Her commissioning officer is Captain S D Mehendale.

Concept note-2: -It is scheduled to be commissioned into service in January NEW DELHI The fifth Scorpène-class conventional submarine, Vagir, was delivered to the Navy by Mazagon Dock Shipbuilders Limited (MDL) Mumbai on December 20. It is scheduled to be commissioned into service next month, a Navy official said.

Concept note-3: -INS Kalvari, the first Scorpene-class submarine built for the Indian Navy, will be fitted with an indigenous Air Independent Propulsion (AIP) system developed by the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), the Ministry of Defence (MoD) has said in a statement.

Concept note-4: -The fifth submarine of the Project – 75, Kalvari Class submarines, Yard 11879 was delivered to the Indian Navy today, 20 Dec 22.

Concept note-5: -Vagir is a Kalvari-class submarine, which includes other vessels, such as the INS Kalvari, INS Khanderi, INS Karanj, INS Vela and INS Vagsheer. Of these, Kalvari and Khanderi were commissioned in 2017 and 2019, and Vela and Karanj were inducted in 2021.
