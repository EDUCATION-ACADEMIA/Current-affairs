How many crores did the Union Government saved through the Direct Benefits Transfer (DBT)?

(A) ** Rs 57, 000

(B) Rs 37, 500

(C) Rs 40, 000

(D) Rs 60, 000

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman on Tuesday said the Union government has saved ₹ 2, 00, 000 crore by using technology as systems like Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) stopped leakages.

Concept note-2: -Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) was launched by the Government of India in January, 2013. Here the money will be directly transferred to the bank account of the beneficiaries of various Government schemes.

Concept note-3: -Government of India launched the Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) Program on 1st Jan 2013 to directly transfer the benefits to the under-privileged population covered under 34 central schemes.

Concept note-4: -The Government of India initially launched the DBT/Direct Benefit Transfer program for 34 central schemes. With the DBT program, the GOI targets to make payments of the subsidies directly to the beneficiaries. This helped in removing any malpractices such as diversions and duplicate payments.

Concept note-5: -The primary aim of this Direct Benefit Transfer program is to bring transparency and terminate pilferage from distribution of funds sponsored by Central Government of India. In DBT, benefit or subsidy will be directly transferred to citizens living below poverty line.
