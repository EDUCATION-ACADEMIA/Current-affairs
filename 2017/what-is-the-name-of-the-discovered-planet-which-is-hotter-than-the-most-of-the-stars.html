What is the name of the discovered planet which is hotter than the most of the stars?

(A) Gliese 411b

(B) Kepler 99

(C) KOI - 500 C

(D) ** KELT-9b

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A newly discovered Jupiter-like world is so hot, it’s being vaporized by its own star. With a dayside temperature of more than 7, 800 degrees Fahrenheit (4, 600 Kelvin), KELT-9b is a planet that is hotter than most stars.

Concept note-2: -Artist’s rendering of a “hot Jupiter” called KELT-9b, the hottest known exoplanet-so hot, a new paper finds, that even molecules in its atmosphere are torn to shreds. In the scorching atmosphere of exoplanet KELT-9b, even molecules are torn to shreds.

Concept note-3: -Venus’ thick atmosphere traps heat creating a runaway greenhouse effect – making it the hottest planet in our solar system with surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead.

Concept note-4: -2. Mercury: The average temperature of Mercury is 167 degree Celsius. It is the first nearest planet from the Sun and the second hottest planet in the solar system.
