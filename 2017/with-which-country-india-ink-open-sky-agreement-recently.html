With Which country India ink Open Sky Agreement recently?

(A) ** Japan

(B) Pakistan

(C) Bulgaria

(D) Australia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The airlines in Canada and India will now have almost limitless rights as a result of the new Open Skies Agreement, after years of being restricted to 35 weekly flights. Now save up to ₹5000 on flight bookings: Use code “INTFLY”.

Concept note-2: -On Monday, Canadian prime minister Justin Trudeau also announced one such agreement for deeper cooperation and ties with India. The agreement is an open-sky agreement between Canada and India that will allow air carriers of both countries to operate unlimited flights between the two countries.

Concept note-3: -The United States and the European Union signed the EU-US Open Skies Agreement in 2007. This agreement, which took effect in March 2008, liberalized the airline industry in the North Atlantic.

Concept note-4: -According to the Open Skies agreement between the U.S. and Japan, travelers may fly on any Japanese air carrier, but ONLY when the following conditions are met: 1. Travel is NOT funded by the U.S. Department of Defense (DOD) including the U.S. Military (Army, Navy and Air Force).
