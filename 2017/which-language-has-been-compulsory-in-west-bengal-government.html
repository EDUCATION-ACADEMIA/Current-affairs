Which language has been compulsory in West Bengal Government?

(A) ** Bengali

(B) Urdu

(C) Tamil

(D) Hindi

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -(1) This Act may be called the West Bengal Official Language Act, 1961. (2) It extends to the whole of West Bengal. (b) elsewhere, the Bengali language, shall be the language or languages to be used for the official purposes of the State of West Bengal referred to in article 345 of the Constitution of India.

Concept note-2: -In Kolkata, students usually opt for only two languages in ICSE, English and either Bengali or Hindi. The third language is offered between Class-VI and Class-VIII. However, if a school offers a choice of two additional languages, one of them has to be Hindi, ”said a school principal.

Concept note-3: -Within India, Bengali is the official language of the states of West Bengal, Tripura and the Barak Valley region of the state of Assam.

Concept note-4: -There are two compulsory language papers. Among them first paper is english and the second paper you can choose it all by yourself.. You don’t have to choose bengali.. But if you get a job as an officer you have to give a bengali paper within next 6-12 months.
