What is the theme for the Zero Discrimination Day 2017

(A) Stand Out

(B) Open Up, Reach Out

(C) ** Make Some Noise for Zero Discrimination

(D) Stand Up

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Organisations like UNAIDS, which fight intolerance against persons living with HIV/AIDS, take special note of the day. In 2017, the UN Development Programme paid homage to LGBTI persons living with HIV/AIDS who encounter harassment. The symbol for Zero Discrimination Day is a butterfly.

Concept note-2: -On Zero Discrimination Day, 1 March, we celebrate the right of everyone to live a full and productive life-and live it with dignity. Zero Discrimination Day highlights how people can become informed about and promote inclusion, compassion, peace and, above all, a movement for change.

Concept note-3: -Zero Discrimination Day 2022-Remove laws that harm, create laws that empower | UNAIDS. On Zero Discrimination Day this year, under the theme “Remove laws that harm, create laws that empower”, UNAIDS is highlighting the urgent need to take action against discriminatory laws.

Concept note-4: -1 March – Zero Discrimination Day The day was launched by UNAIDS Executive Director Michel Sidibe and was celebrated on 1st March 2014. The day is observed every year with a specific theme to achieve its objective. The theme for Zero Discrimination Day 2022 was “Remove laws that harm, create laws that empower”.
