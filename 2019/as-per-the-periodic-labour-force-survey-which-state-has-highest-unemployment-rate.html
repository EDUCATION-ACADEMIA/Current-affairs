As per the ‘Periodic Labour Force Survey’ which state has highest unemployment rate?

(A) ** Nagaland

(B) Odisha

(C) Bihar

(D) Arunachal Pradesh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What 5 states have the highest unemployment? Haryana, Rajasthan, Delhi, Bihar, and Jharkhand have the highest unemployment rate in India as of December 2022.

Concept note-2: -While the urban unemployment rate had increased to 10.09% in December from 8.96% in the previous month, the rural unemployment rate slipped to 7.44% from 7.55%, the data on the CMIE website showed. Haryana recorded the highest unemployment rate at 37.4%, while Odisha recorded the lowest at 0.9% last month.

Concept note-3: -Total unemployment rate Haryana is the top region by unemployment rate in India. As of last month, unemployment rate in Haryana was 37.38 %. The top 5 regions also includes Rajasthan, NCT of Delhi, Bihar, and Jharkhand. The description is composed by our digital data assistant.

Concept note-4: -The figures on the CMIE website indicated that while the urban unemployment rate rose to 10.09% in December from 8.96% the previous month, the rural jobless rate decreased to 7.44% from 7.55%. The highest unemployment rate in India State Wise 2022 last month was 37.4% in Haryana, while the lowest was 0.9% in Odisha.

Concept note-5: -Meghalaya is the top region by labor force participation rate in India. As of last month, labor force participation rate in Meghalaya was 62.11 %. The top 5 regions also includes Sikkim, Tripura, Haryana, and Assam.
