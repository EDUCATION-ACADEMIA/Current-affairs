The reservation for SC/STs in Lok Sabha, state assemblies has been extended by how much period?

(A) 15 years

(B) 12 years

(C) ** 10 years

(D) 5 years

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Article 334 of the Constitution had originally required the reservation of elected seats to cease in 1960, but this was extended to 1970 by the 8th Amendment. The period of reservation was extended to 1980, 1990, 2000 and 2010 by the 23rd, 45th, 62nd and 79th Amendments respectively.

Concept note-2: -Some seats are reserved in Lok Sabha for the members of the Schedule Castes and Scheduled Tribes. As per the order issued by the Delimitation Commission in 2008 , 412 are general, 84 seats are reserved for Scheduled Castes and 47 seats for the Scheduled Tribes .

Concept note-3: -Article 334 grants reservations in the Lok Sabha and State Legislatures to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, and the Anglo-Indian community.

Concept note-4: -The Lok Sabha has 84 seats reserved for the Scheduled Castes and 47 seats for the Scheduled Tribes.
