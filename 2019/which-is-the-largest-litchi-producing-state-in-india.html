Which is the largest ‘Litchi’ Producing state in India?

(A) ** Bihar

(B) West Bengal

(C) Uttar Pradesh

(D) Punjab

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bihar- Bihar accounts for 40 percent of the National production. It is grown largely in the districts of Muzaffarpur which is the litchi bowl of India, Vaishali, Samastipur, and East Champaran falls under the same districts for Litchi cultivation.

Concept note-2: -It is grown in the states of Bihar, Tripura, West Bengal, Uttar Pradesh, Punjab and Haryana. Of the total production of lychee in India, 74 percent is contributed by Bihar. The second largest lychee producing state is West Bengal followed by Tripura and Assam (Table 2).

Concept note-3: -India is the second largest producer of litchi in the World after China. Other major producing countries are Thailand, Australia, South Africa, Madagascar and Florida in US.

Concept note-4: -Bihar’s litchi production was 74% of India’s production. Bihar is the leading state in litchi production followed by West Bengal and Assam.

Concept note-5: -Muzaffarpur District and surrounding areas are world famous for its Shahi Litchis. Here hundreds of Litchi Gardens. Litchis produced near the area of river Budi Gandak has better quality due to the right climate for this crop.
