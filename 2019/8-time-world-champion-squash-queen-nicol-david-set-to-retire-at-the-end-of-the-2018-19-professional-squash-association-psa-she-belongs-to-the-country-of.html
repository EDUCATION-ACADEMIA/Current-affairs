8-time world champion, Squash queen Nicol David set to retire at the end of the 2018-19 Professional Squash Association (PSA). She belongs to the country of;

(A) Italy

(B) Russia

(C) Germany

(D) ** Malaysia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nicol David, (born August 26, 1983, Penang, Malaysia), Malaysian squash player who dominated the sport in the early 21st century and became the first to win eight World Open crowns (2005–06, 2008–12, 2014). David’s interest in squash started at the age of five.

Concept note-2: -The former world squash champion together with Colombian professional squash professional Mariana de Reyes recently launched the Nicol David Organisation, located in Bukit Jalil, Kuala Lumpur, to “empower girls and boys through sport and education”.

Concept note-3: -She has 81 WISPA & 8 World Championship titles under her belt. One of the greatest achievement of Nicol’s career as a squash player is the sheer number of world titles she has won. She has clinched 81 Women’s International Squash Players Association (WISPA) titles and eight World Championship titles.

Concept note-4: -David, now 38, began playing squash at the age of five in Penang. In 1999 the 15-year-old David became the youngest woman to win a World Junior Championship. She turned professional in 2000 and within three years she moved home from Penang to join Irving in Amsterdam.
