Defence Ministry has awarded ______ to build 8 Anti-Submarine Warfare Shallow Watercraft For Indian Navy.

(A) Mazagon Dock Limited, Mumbai

(B) Hindustan Shipyard Limited, Visakhapatnam

(C) ** Garden Reach Shipbuilders and Engineers, Kolkata

(D) Cochin Shipyard Limited

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Indian Navy launched ‘Arnala’, the first of the eight indigenously-built anti-submarine warfare shallow water craft (ASW-SWC), at the shipbuilding facility of L&T in Chennai’s Kattupall. Built by Garden Reach Shipbuilders and Engineers (GRSE), ‘Arnala’ made its first contact with the water of the Bay of Bengal.

Concept note-2: -Under this ‘Project-75 India’, the six submarines will have land-attack cruise missiles as well as AIP. But it will take almost a decade from now for the first such submarine to roll out.

Concept note-3: -Arnala class of ships will replace the Abhay class ASW Ships of the Indian Navy and are designed to undertake anti-submarine operations in coastal waters and Low Intensity Maritime Operations including subsurface surveillance in littoral waters.

Concept note-4: -Keel laying of the two warships (Yard 3033 and Yard 3036) of Anti-submarine Warfare Shallow Craft (ASW SWC) project under construction by GRSE, Kolkata, was undertaken on 31 Dec 22 by Shri Giridhar Aramane, IAS, Defence Secretary.
