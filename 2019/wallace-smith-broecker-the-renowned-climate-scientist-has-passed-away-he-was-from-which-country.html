Wallace Smith Broecker, the renowned Climate scientist has passed away. He was from which country?

(A) Germany

(B) ** United States

(C) France

(D) Italy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Wallace “Wally” Smith Broecker (November 29, 1931 – February 18, 2019) was an American geochemist.

Concept note-2: -He was 87. The cause was congestive heart failure, said his family. His death was confirmed by Columbia University’s Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory, where he spent a career that spanned nearly 67 years.

Concept note-3: -Broecker. To his colleagues, peers and admirers he is a genius and a pioneer, the Grandfather of Climate Science. And to his countless friends – most of whom also happen to be colleagues, peers and admirers – he is simply Wally.

Concept note-4: -In 1896, a seminal paper by Swedish scientist Svante Arrhenius first predicted that changes in atmospheric carbon dioxide levels could substantially alter the surface temperature through the greenhouse effect. In 1938, Guy Callendar connected carbon dioxide increases in Earth’s atmosphere to global warming.

Concept note-5: -The term “global warming,” which describes an increase in the Earth’s average temperature surface due to greenhouse gas emissions, is widely believed to have been coined in 1975 by Columbia University geochemist Wallace Broecker, according to NASA.
