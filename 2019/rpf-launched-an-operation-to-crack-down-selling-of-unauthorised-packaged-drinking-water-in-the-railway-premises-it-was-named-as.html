RPF launched an operation to crack down selling of unauthorised Packaged Drinking Water in the railway premises. It was named as;

(A) Operation Phani

(B) Operation Water Baby

(C) ** Operation Thirst

(D) Operation Clean Water

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Operation Thirst: launched by Railway Protection Force.

Concept note-2: -In 1965 it has been renamed as “Railway Protection Special Force”. In 1966 RPF has been given legal powers for better protection of Railway property by enacting Railway Property (Unlawful Possession.) Act.

Concept note-3: -Indian Railways have launched “Operation Thirst”, as an all India drive to curb the menace of selling unauthorized packaged drinking water in railway premises.

Concept note-4: -UPSC Mains. Q. How is the Railway Protection Force (RPF) recently renamed by the Ministry of Railways? Notes: The Ministry of Railways recently granted ‘Organized Group A Status’ (OGAS) to the Railway Protection Force (RPF) and also renamed it as the Indian Railway Protection Force Service.
