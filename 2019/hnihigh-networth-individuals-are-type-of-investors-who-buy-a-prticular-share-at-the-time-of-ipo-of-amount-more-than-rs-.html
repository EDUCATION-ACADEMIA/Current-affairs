HNI(HIgh Networth Individuals) are type of Investors who buy a prticular share at the time of IPO of amount more than Rs ____

(A) 100000

(B) ** 200000

(C) 300000

(D) 400000

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -HNIs or high net-worth individuals (HNIs) belong to the financial services sector where a class of individuals has an investible surplus of more than Rs 5 crore, below this threshold.

Concept note-2: -HNI stands for High Networth Individuals. This is a separate category specified in IPO investment under the Non-Institutional Investors(NII) section. This section also includes NRIs, HUFs, FPIs, Trusts and Companies. HNI IPO application is a part of the NII portion of the IPO allotment.

Concept note-3: -IPO allotment is done on a lottery or proportionate basis depending on the status of oversubscription in the segment. The shares will get allotted within six working days from the offer closing day. There is no lock-in period for the HNI category like QIB.
