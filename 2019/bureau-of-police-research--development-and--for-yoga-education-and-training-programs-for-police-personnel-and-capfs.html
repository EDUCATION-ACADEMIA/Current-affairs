Bureau of Police Research & Development and ____ for Yoga education and training programs for Police Personnel and CAPFs.

(A) The Lonavla Yoga Institute

(B) Swasthya Siddhi Yoga

(C) Kaivalyadhama Yoga Institute

(D) ** MDNIY

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Bureau of Police Research & Development (BPR&D) was set up in 1970 to identify the needs and problems of the police in the country, undertake research projects and studies, and suggest modalities to overcome problems and challenges and meet the needs and requirements of the police.

Concept note-2: -Bureau of Police Research and Development [BPRD] Objective The objective of the Bureau of Police Research and Development is to identify the problems of police forces operating in the country, conduct studies and research, and suggest effective solutions to overcome the issues or meet the needs of the forces.

Concept note-3: -What is the Selection Procedure for BPRD? The Selection Procedure for is Prelims Exam, Mains Exam and Interview. Candidates qualifying in the prelims exam will be eligible to appear for the mains exam. Those candidates who qualify in the mains exam will be eligible to appear for the interview which is the final stage.

Concept note-4: -Although there are variations across the country, there are three core types of police training: (1) basic training, (2) field training, and (3) in-service training.
