How many states are directed by the National Green Tribunal to submit action plans for bringing air quality standards?

(A) ** 6

(B) 4

(C) 8

(D) 12

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Under the authority of the Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act of 1981, Indiaâ€™s Central Pollution Control Board sets national ambient air quality standards and is responsible for both testing air quality and assisting governments in planning to meet such standards.

Concept note-2: -New Delhi is the Principal Place of Sitting of the Tribunal and Bhopal, Pune, Kolkata and Chennai shall be the other four place of sitting of the Tribunal.

Concept note-3: -The higher the AQI value, the greater the level of air pollution and the greater the health concern. For example, an AQI value of 50 or below represents good air quality, while an AQI value over 300 represents hazardous air quality.

Concept note-4: -National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS) are standards for air quality that are set by the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) that are applicable all over the country. Topics related to the environment, especially, air pollution/quality are essential for the IAS exam.
