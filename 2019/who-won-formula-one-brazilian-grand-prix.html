Who won Formula One Brazilian Grand Prix?

(A) Lewis Hamilton

(B) ** Max Verstappen

(C) Sebastian Vettel

(D) Charles Leclerc

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -George Russell wins the Brazilian grand prix! Safety car! Verstappen gets a five-second time penalty for causing a collision! Contact between Hamilton and Verstappen!

Concept note-2: -Mercedes driver George Russell took his maiden Grand Prix win with his team mate Lewis Hamilton in second and with Ferrari driver Carlos Sainz Jr. completing the podium in third. This race marked the first time since the 2010 Canadian Grand Prix that British drivers finished first and second in a Grand Prix.

Concept note-3: -Winner: George Russell George Russell couldn’t hold back the tears as he celebrated a maiden Grand Prix victory with an accomplished drive – which included two faultless Safety Car restarts from the lead – at Interlagos.

Concept note-4: -George Russell claimed a maiden Formula 1 win in the Brazilian Grand Prix - here are the full results from a thriller at Interlagos. George Russell took his first Formula 1 in the Brazilian Grand Prix, holding off Mercedes teammate Lewis Hamilton on a late restart.

Concept note-5: -Emerson Fittipaldi spent ten years in Formula One and won the Drivers’ Championship in 1972 and 1974. The Autosport driver survey placed Fittipaldi in 12th place, one ahead of Piquet. Fittipaldi joined Formula One in 1970 with Lotus and achieved one victory in his first two years.
