‘Ranthambhore National Park’ is in which state?

(A) Madhya Pradesh

(B) Chattisgarh

(C) Haryana

(D) ** Rajasthan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ranthambore National Park is a national park in Rajasthan, India, with an area of 1,334 km2 (515 sq mi).

Concept note-2: -km is the core zone and 118 sq. km is the buffer zone, the Ranthambore tiger reserve park is located in the Sawai Madhopur district of Rajasthan state of India. As the Ranthambore forest is the reserved tiger park, The Indian tiger or the Bengal tiger (Panthera Tigris) is the prime and most revered species in the park.

Concept note-3: -Ranthambore Fort lies within the Ranthambore National Park, near the city of Sawai Madhopur in Sawai Madhopur district of Rajasthan, India.

Concept note-4: -Ranthambore National Park was established initially as Sawai Madhopur Game Sanctuary in 1955 by the Government of India. In 1973, it was declared as one of the Project Tiger reserves in India.

Concept note-5: -The nearest town and railway station of Ranthambore is Sawai Madhopur which is about 14 kilometers from Ranthambore Park. Sawai Madhopur railway station is connected to all major cities like Jaipur, Agra, Bharatpur, Mumbai, Vadodara and Delhi.
