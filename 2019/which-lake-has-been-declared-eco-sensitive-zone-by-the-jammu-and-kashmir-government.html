Which lake has been declared Eco-Sensitive Zone by the Jammu and Kashmir Government?

(A) Mansar Lake

(B) Wular Lake

(C) ** Dal Lake

(D) Nigeen Lake

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In November, the Jammu and Kashmir administration had set up a 10-member committee to declare the Dal Lake and its surrounding areas an eco-sensitive zone, following concerns over its shrinking size.

Concept note-2: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Dal Lake, Purmandal Lake and Wular Lake. Dal Lake in Srinagar along with five other famous lakes in Jammu and Kashmir would be declared protected wetlands.

Concept note-3: -Dal Lake, Wular Lake, Nigeen Lake, Mansar Lake, Gadsar Lake, Manasbal Lake, Gangabal Lake, Kausar Nag Lake, Vishanshar Lake and many more.

Concept note-4: -For Mains: Significance and the need for these zones, and issues related to management of these zones. Context: The Jammu and Kashmir UT will set up a 10-member committee that will declare Dal Lake, an Eco-sensitive Zone.

Concept note-5: -Anchar Lake is a lake located near Soura area, near the city of Srinagar in Srinagar district of Kashmir, India. Situated close to Ganderbal, the lake is connected with the famous Dal Lake via a channel “Amir Khan Nallah” which passes through Gilsar and Khushal Sar.
