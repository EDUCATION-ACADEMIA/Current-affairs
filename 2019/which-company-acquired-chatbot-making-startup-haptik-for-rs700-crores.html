Which company acquired Chatbot Making Startup Haptik for Rs.700 crores?

(A) Tech Mahindra

(B) Aditya Birla Group

(C) Hathway

(D) ** Reliance Jio Digital

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the ₹700-crore deal, ₹230 crore will go for business transfer and ₹470 crore will go towards expansion. Haptik Co-founders Aakrit Vaish and Swapan Rajdev and employees will have a minority stake in Reliance Jio.

Concept note-2: -Haptik is a part of Jio Platforms Limited (JPL), who invested $100 million into the company in April 2019. Jio Platforms is a $60 billion internet conglomerate that reaches 500 million consumers across Telecom, E-Commerce, Financial Services, Digital Media, etc. and counts Facebook as one of its marquee investors.

Concept note-3: -Haptik’s repertoire of chatbot customers in India includes Samsung, Future Group, KFC, Dream11, Sharekhan, Edelweiss, Tokio, Club Mahindra and IIFL among others. Haptik has also built assistants for TOI, Samsung, Ziman and Akancha Against Harassment, an online Cyber Safety Initiative.

Concept note-4: -Responding to this, Mukesh Ambani, owner of Jio, said, “All operators have publicly said last week that they will provide this (interconnect and MNP).

Concept note-5: -Global headquarters for Haptik is located in Mumbai,Maharashtra,India.
