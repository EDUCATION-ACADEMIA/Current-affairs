Which Bay is the largest Bay in the world?

(A) Bay of Bengal

(B) ** Hudson Bay

(C) Bay of Fundy

(D) Chesapeake Bay

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Bay of Bengal, the largest bay in the world, was formed by plate tectonics.

Concept note-2: -The Hudson Bay encompasses 1,230,000 square kilometers, making it the second largest bay in the world after the Bay of Bengal (2,172,000 square kilometers).

Concept note-3: -Notes: The Bay of Bengal is the largest bay in the world in the terms of area. It is in the northeastern part of the Indian Ocean. It encompasses an area of 2,172,000 square km, making it the largest water body called a bay in the world.

Concept note-4: -It is the largest water region called a bay in the world. There are countries dependent on the Bay of Bengal in South Asia and Southeast Asia. During the existence of British India, it was named the Bay of Bengal after the historic Bengal region.

Concept note-5: -The largest bays have developed through plate tectonics.
