Who has become the first Indian-origin woman to trek solo to the South Pole?

(A) Srishti Bandla

(B) Sneha Sharma

(C) Sunita Williams

(D) ** Harpreet Chandi

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Liv Arnesen of Norway is the first woman to ski alone to the South Pole.

Concept note-2: -Captain Harpreet Chandi, a 32-year-old Indian-origin British Sikh Army officer and physiotherapist also known as Polar Preet, has created history by becoming the first woman of colour to complete a solo unsupported trek to the South Pole.

Concept note-3: -Colonel J.K. Bajaj became the first Indian to reach the South Pole at a 0-degree latitude in Antarctica, South Pole.
