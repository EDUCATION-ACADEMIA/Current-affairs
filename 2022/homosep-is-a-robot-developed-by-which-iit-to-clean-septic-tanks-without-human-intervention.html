HomoSEP is a robot, developed by which IIT to clean septic tanks without human intervention?

(A) IIT Delhi

(B) ** IIT Madras

(C) IIT Kanpur

(D) IIT Bombay

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -IIT Madras’ Robot to clean Septic Tanks without human intervention set for Field Deployment. Indian Institute of Technology Madras’ ‘HomoSEP,’ a Robot developed by the Institute’s Researchers to eliminate manual scavenging in India, is all set for field deployment.

Concept note-2: -HomoSEP robot has been developed by a team led by Prof. Prabhu Rajagopal from IIT Madras, in association with IIT Madras-incubated start-up Solinas Integrity Private Limited. It is also supported by Safai Karamchari Andolan (SKA) NGO, which is dedicated towards eliminating manual scavenging in India.

Concept note-3: -The IIT Madras has developed a robot that can clean septic tanks without the need for humans. Ten units are expected to be distributed across Tamil Nadu. The Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras has developed a robot that can clean septic tanks without the need for humans.

Concept note-4: -IIT Madras has deveoped HomoSep, a robot to clean septic tanks. The robots are invented to stop manual scavenging. Several government and private organisations have helped this project become reality.

Notes: Researchers at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Madras have developed a robot to clean septic tanks without human intervention. Named HomoSEP, the product development was further supported by GAIL (India).
