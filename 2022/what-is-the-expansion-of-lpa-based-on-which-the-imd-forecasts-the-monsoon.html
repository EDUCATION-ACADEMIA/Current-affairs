What is the expansion of ‘LPA’ based on which the IMD forecasts the monsoon?

(A) Least period average

(B) Least prime average

(C) Long prime average

(D) ** Long period average

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -According to the IMD, the “LPA of rainfall is the rainfall recorded over a particular region for a given interval (like month or season) average over a long period like 30 years, 50 years, etc”.

Concept note-2: -Explanation: Monsoon rainfall is likely to be “normal” at 99% of long period average (LPA) with an error margin of +/-5%, India Meteorological Department (IMD) said in its long-range forecast.

Concept note-3: -Lakhs Per Annum (LPA) is a unit of Cost to Company (CTC) in India. 1 Lakh is Rs. 1,00,000.

Concept note-4: -What is the Range of normal rainfall in the country? As per IMD, southwest monsoon seasonal (June to September) rainfall over the country as a whole is most likely to be normal (96 to 104% of LPA.

Concept note-5: -Prediction of monsoon is done by IMD in different spatial and temporal scales. It varies from country as a whole to district wise in spatial scales and from a seasonal forecast to nowcast in temporal scale.
