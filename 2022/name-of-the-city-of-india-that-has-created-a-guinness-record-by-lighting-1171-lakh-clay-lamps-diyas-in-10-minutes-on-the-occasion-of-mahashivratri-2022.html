Name of the city of India that has created a Guinness record by lighting 11.71 lakh clay lamps (diyas) in 10 minutes, on the occasion of Mahashivratri 2022?

(A) Haridwar

(B) ** Ujjain

(C) Delhi

(D) Varanasi

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Madhya Pradesh Government on Tuesday set a new Guinness world record of ‘largest display of oil lamps ’ by lighting more than 11.71 lakh lamps in Ujjain as part of the Mahashivratri celebrations. The city thus broke the record set in Ayodhya last year, officials said.

Concept note-2: -Over 15.76 lakh earthen lamps were lit one by one with the chanting of ‘Shri Ram Jai Ram Jai Jai Ram’ as soon as the PM launched the festival. As the representatives of the Guinness Book of World Records announced the establishment of the record, the entire city of Ayodhya echoed with the chants of ‘Jai Shri Ram.

Concept note-3: -This year, the people of Ayodhya managed to create a world record by doing so. For Ayodhya Deepotsav 2022, 19,000 volunteers on the occasion of Chotti Diwali, managed to light 17 lakh diyas on the Ram Ki Paidi on the banks of the revered Saryu River.

Concept note-4: -Breaking its own record, Ayodhya bagged the Guinness World record for the ‘largest display of oil lamps’ by lighting more than 15.76 lakh earthen lamps at Ram ki Paidi during the last day of the Deepotsav celebrations on Sunday.

Concept note-5: -More than 17 lakh earthen diyas lit up, presenting a mesmerising view on the banks of Saryu river at Ayodhya, it seemed like heaven on earth. This also broke the record of lighting the most diyas on Diwali in Ayodhya.
