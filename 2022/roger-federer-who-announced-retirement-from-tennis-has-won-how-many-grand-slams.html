Roger Federer, who announced retirement from Tennis, has won how many Grand Slams?

(A) 18

(B) ** 20

(C) 10

(D) 15

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Widely regarded as one of the greatest to ever pick up a racket, Federer retires with 20 Grand Slam singles titles, behind Novak Djokovic (21) and Rafael Nadal (22), as well as winning Olympic gold in doubles at Beijing 2008 and silver in singles at London 2012.

Concept note-2: -Federer has won 20 Grand Slam men’s singles titles, third behind Djokovic (22) and Nadal (22). He was the first male player to win more than 14 Grand Slams.

Concept note-3: -The 2018 Australian Open was his record-extending 20th Grand Slam trophy and ultimately his last. “During the match I constantly thought about the fact that I could reach 20,” Federer said. “I was nervous the whole day, I thought about what would happen if I lose, if I win. That’s why I broke down during the speech.”

Concept note-4: -Roger Federer announces retirement from professional tennis – DW – 09/15/2022.
