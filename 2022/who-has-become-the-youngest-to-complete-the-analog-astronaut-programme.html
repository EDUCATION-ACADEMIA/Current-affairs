Who has become the youngest to complete the Analog Astronaut programme?

(A) Anil Menon

(B) ** Jahnavi Dangeti

(C) Raja Chari

(D) Sirisha Bandla

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nineteen-year-old Jahnavi Dangeti has scripted history by becoming the youngest to complete the Analog Astronaut programme from the Analog Astronaut Training Centre (AATC) at Krakow in South Poland.

Concept note-2: -Jahnavi is the youngest among the batch, titled ASTRA-45. In 2021, Ms Jahnavi had completed NASAâ€™s International Air and Space Programme (IASP) at the Kennedy Space Centra in the U.S.

Concept note-3: -Who is analog astronaut? Analog astronaut is an individual that decided to play the role of astronaut during a simulated crewed mission. Depending on the study and environment a different requirement could be applied for a candidate for analog astronaut.

Concept note-4: -There are no age restrictions for the program. Astronaut candidates selected in the past have ranged between the ages of 26 and 46, with the average age being 34.

Concept note-5: -Age 30 to 55 years. Successful completion of a modified U.S. Air Force Class III Physical, dental and oral examination, and psychological screening. Willingness to be confined and isolated for extended periods of time. English and Russian verbal and written proficiency.
