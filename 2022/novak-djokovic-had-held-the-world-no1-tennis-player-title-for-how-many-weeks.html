Novak Djokovic had held the world no.1 Tennis Player title for how many weeks?

(A) 365 weeks

(B) ** 361 weeks

(C) 371 weeks

(D) 357 weeks

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Novak Djokovic has won an all-time record 22 Grand Slam men’s singles titles, jointly-held with Rafael Nadal. He has been the world No. 1 for a record 375 weeks, and the year-end world No. 1 a record seven times.

Concept note-2: -Novak Djokovic has spent the most weeks as world No. 1, a record total 375 weeks. Roger Federer has the record of 237 consecutive weeks at No. 1. Djokovic also holds the record for the most year-end No. 1 rankings, achieving the feat for seven years (including the pandemic-shortened season).

Concept note-3: -LONGEST STREAKS AT NO. 1 IN ATP RANKINGS HISTORY (since 1973): 237 weeks: Roger Federer [February 2, 2004 to August 17, 2008] 160 weeks: Jimmy Connors [July 29, 1974 to August 22, 1977]

Concept note-4: -He later won the 2019 U.S. Open singles title, which was his 19th career Grand Slam championship, the second most for men behind Federer’s 20 titles. The following year he tied Federer when he won his 13th French Open title. (Their record was equaled in July 2021 by Djokovic.)
