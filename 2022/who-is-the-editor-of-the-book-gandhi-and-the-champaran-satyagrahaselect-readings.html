Who is the editor of the book “Gandhi and the Champaran Satyagraha:Select Readings”?

(A) Sanjeev Tripathi

(B) Dipesh Mishra

(C) ** Suranjan Das

(D) Vikram Joshi

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A book titled Gandhi and the Champaran Satyagraha: Select Readings, which has been edited by Jadavpur University Vice-Chancellor Suranjan Das, was released on Saturday.

Concept note-2: -Gandhi and The Champaran Satyagraha: Select Readings by Suranjan Das - PrimusBooks.

Concept note-3: -Gandhiji originally wrote his experiences of Satyagraha in South Africa in great detail in Gujarati, and the first English edition of the book was published in 1928.

Concept note-4: -Mahatma Gandhi played a crucial role in the Champaran Satyagraha. He launched the first satyagraha movement in India in order to fight against the injustice meted out to the peasants of Champaran.

Concept note-5: -Ans. The Champaran Satyagraha was a peasants uprising, led by Mahatma Gandhi in the Champaran district of Bihar. The farmer’s in the region revolted against the British power against the imposed indigo cultivation between 1914 and 1916.
