Which Indian state is the first to set up a Gene Bank Project?

(A) Kerala

(B) ** Maharashtra

(C) West Bengal

(D) Telangana

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Maharashtra Cabinet on Thursday approved the country’s first-of-its-kind gene bank programme in the State. The programme is aimed at conserving native biodiversity to ensure that natural resources are passed on to the next generation.

Concept note-2: -The National Gene Bank established in the year 1996 to preserve the seeds of Plant Genetic Resources (PGR) for future generations, has the capacity to preserve about one million germplasm in the form of seeds.

Concept note-3: -Recently, Maharashtra Cabinet approved the ‘Maharashtra Gene Bank’, a first-of-its-kind project in India.

Concept note-4: -It is headquartered in New Delhi and has 10 regional stations.

Concept note-5: -The International Crops Research Institute for the Semi-Arid Tropics (ICRISAT) in Hyderabad has set up the world’s first gene bank that is powered entirely by solar energy. Spread across 4,500 sq metres, the gene bank is powered by a 500 kW solar power plant generating 60,000 units per month.
