A special loan scheme ‘Jeevala’ has been launched by which state government?

(A) Odisha

(B) Karnataka

(C) Chhattisgarh

(D) ** Maharashtra

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Q. ‘Jivhala’ is a special loan scheme launched by which state government? Notes: The Maharashtra Department of Prisons has launched first kind of credit scheme for prisoners in jails across the state.

Concept note-2: -Which bank is offering this scheme? Maharashtra State Cooperative Bank is offering this newly launched credit scheme that has been named Jivhala. This credit scheme is a first-of-its-kind scheme that has been launched for prisoners in India.

Concept note-3: -The credit scheme, named Jivhala, which means affection in Marathi, is primarily for inmates who are undergoing a prison sentence of more than three years. In the initial phase of this scheme, a Rs 50,000 loan will be given. The interest rate that will be applicable is 7%.

Concept note-4: -Mumbai (Maharashtra): Maharashtra government on Tuesday launched a scheme to provide personal loans for prisoners with the aim to improve the living standard of the families of the inmates.

Concept note-5: -Also Check Karnataka Raitha Siri Scheme 2022 Also in urban areas, the Karnataka government will pay 6.5 % interest on the home loans for up to 12 Lakh Rupees. While in rural areas, the government has going to pay 8% interest on the home loans of up to 6 Lakh Rupees.
