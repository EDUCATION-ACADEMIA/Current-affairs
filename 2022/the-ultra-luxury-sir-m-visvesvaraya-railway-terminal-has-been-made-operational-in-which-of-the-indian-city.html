The ultra-luxury Sir M Visvesvaraya Railway Terminal has been made operational in which of the Indian city?

(A) ** Bengaluru

(B) Hyderabad

(C) Indore

(D) Varanasi

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The ultra luxury Sir M Visvesvaraya Railway Terminal in Karnataka’s capital city Bengaluru was made operational. The Ernakulam Tri-Weekly Express chugged trough the station to mark this special occasion. According to the railway officials, the air-conditioned SMV Railway Terminal is a Rs 314 crore project.

Concept note-2: -Visvesvaraya Terminal, Bengaluru railway station (station code: SMVB) is an Indian Railways train station located in Baiyyapanahalli, Bangalore in the Indian state of Karnataka.

Concept note-3: -#Bengaluru gets India’s first ultra-luxury railway terminal with airport-like facilities . The Sir M Visvesvaraya Terminal at Baiyappanahalli is built at the cost of Rs 314 crore and is open for public use now. The railway terminal is modelled after the Kempegowda International Airport in Bengaluru.

Concept note-4: -Some of the popular terminal stations in India are Anand Vihar Terminal (ANVT), Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (CST), Lokmanya Tilak Terminus (LTT), Bandra Terminus (BDTS), and Rajendra Nagar Terminus (RJPB).

Concept note-5: -“Sir M Visvesvaraya is India’s first centralised AC Terminal, a benchmark for future Railway terminals.
