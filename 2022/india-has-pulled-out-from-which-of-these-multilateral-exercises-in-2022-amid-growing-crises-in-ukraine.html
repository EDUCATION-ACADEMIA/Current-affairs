India has pulled out from which of these multilateral exercises in 2022, amid growing crises in Ukraine?

(A) ** Cobra Warrior

(B) Sea Breeze

(C) Shared Destiny

(D) Desert Knight

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -India-France Garuda Exercise 2022 IAF and FASF have been holding Garuda series of air exercises since the first in 2003 at Air Force Station Jodhpur, India. Subsequently, they were held in 2005, 2006, 2010, 2014 and 2019. The latest in 2022 was held in Jodhpur.

Concept note-2: -The military exercise between Russia and India is known as Indira. The INDRA series of exercises began in 2003 and was conducted as a bilateral naval exercise alternately between the two countries. The 2021 edition of Indra took place on August 4, at Prudboy Ranges in Volgograd, Russia.

Concept note-3: -The exercise ‘Veer Guardian-2023’ involving the Indian Air Force (IAF) and Japan Air Self Defence Force (JASDF) will be held at Japan’s Hyakuri air base. The IAF said on Saturday that its deployment at the exercise will include four Su-30 MKI jets, two C-17 aircraft and one IL-78 plane.

Concept note-4: -21st EDITION OF INDIA FRANCE BILATERAL NAVAL EXERCISE ‘VARUNA’ – 2023. The 21st Edition of the Bilateral Naval Exercise between India and France – Exercise Varuna commenced on the Western Seaboard today, 16 Jan 23.
