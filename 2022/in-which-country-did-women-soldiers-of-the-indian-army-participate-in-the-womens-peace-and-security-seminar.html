In which country did women soldiers of the Indian army participate in the women’s peace and security seminar?

(A) ** Mongolia

(B) Germany

(C) Russia

(D) France

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Women soldiers of the Indian Army participated in the four-day ‘Women Peace and Security’ Seminar at Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia.

Concept note-2: -Shanti Tigga was the first female jawan in the Indian Army. Her fitness and skill surpassed that of her male colleagues, following which she was awarded the title of Best Trainee in the Recruitment Training Camp.

Concept note-3: -The Rani of Jhansi Regiment was the women’s regiment of the Indian National Army, the armed force formed by Indian nationalists in 1942 in Southeast Asia with the aim of overthrowing the British Raj in colonial India, with Japanese assistance.

Concept note-4: -The role of women in the Indian Armed Forces was further expanded with the formation of the Women’s Auxiliary Corps, which allowed them to serve in primarily non-combatant roles like communications, accounting, administration etc.

Concept note-5: -Captain Shiva Chauhan: Meet the first woman Army officer operationally deployed at the forbidding Siachen Glacier | India News - Times of India.
