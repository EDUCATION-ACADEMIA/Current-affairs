What per cent stake will be acquired by NTPC in Power Exchange of India Ltd?

(A) ** 5%

(B) 3%

(C) 4%

(D) 6%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As per industry data, short-term trading is around 14-15 per cent of total power supply in India and most of that is bilateral PPAs (power purchase agreements) while the exchange trading is 5 per cent. The government intends to increase this 5 per cent to 25 per cent by 2023-24.

Concept note-2: -NEW DELHI : NTPC Vidyut Vyapar Nigam Ltd (NVVN), a wholly-owned subsidiary of state-run power major NTPC, has acquired a 5% equity stake in Power Exchange of India (PXIL).

Concept note-3: -NTPC is committed to generating power responsibly and sustainably. Besides fossil fuels the company has diversified into producing energy through cleaner and greener sources such as hydro and solar energy. NTPC occupies the premier position in the Indian energy sector in terms of size and efficiency.

Concept note-4: -forecast annual revenue growth Earnings vs Savings Rate: NTPC’s forecast earnings growth (7.6% per year) is above the savings rate (6.8%). Earnings vs Market: NTPC’s earnings (7.6% per year) are forecast to grow slower than the Indian market (18.8% per year).
