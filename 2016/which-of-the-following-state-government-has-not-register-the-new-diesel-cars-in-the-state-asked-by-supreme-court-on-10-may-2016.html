Which of the following state government has not register the new diesel cars in the state asked by Supreme Court on 10 May 2016

(A) Madhya Pradesh

(B) Uttar Pradesh

(C) ** New Delhi

(D) Bihar

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Chaitanya Singh. The Supreme Court on Tuesday imposed costs of ₹50, 000 on two petitioners who moved the Court challenging the ban in Delhi on diesel vehicles older than 10 years and petrol vehicles older than 15 years [Anurag Saxena and Anr. v. Union of India].

Concept note-2: -The decision was taken in a state Cabinet meeting chaired by Chief Minister Manohar Lal Khattar. “Under the policy, the vehicles which have completed the critical age of 10 and 15 years in case of diesel and petrol variants, respectively shall be scrapped.

Concept note-3: -10-year diesels permanently banned The rule of the National Green Tribunal states that the use of 10-year old diesel-powered and 15-year old petrol-powered cars is not allowed in New Delhi.

Concept note-4: -In its order, the top court of the country mandated the Transport Department of Delhi for a complete ban on plying of 10-year old diesel vehicles and 15-year old petrol vehicles on the roads within Delhi and the National Capital Region.
