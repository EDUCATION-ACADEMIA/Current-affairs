India’s largest coal washery will be set up in which state

(A) Maharastra

(B) Jharkhand

(C) Uttar Pradesh

(D) ** Chhattisgarh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -India’s largest coal washery will be set up in which state? The South Eastern Coalfields Limited (SECL), a subsidiary of the Coal India Limited (CIL), will set up India’s largest coal washery with a capacity of 25 million tonnes per annum (MTPA) in Korba district of Chhattisgarh.

Concept note-2: -Jharkhand has the highest coal reserves in India.

Concept note-3: -The largest coal producing state in India is Jharkhand, followed by Odisha and Chhattisgarh. In this state, there are approximately 83.15 billion tonnes of coal reserves.

Concept note-4: -India’s coal production is concentrated in various regions of West Bengal, Jharkhand, Odisha, and Chattisgarh. The largest coalfield is situated in Jharia of Jharkhand.
