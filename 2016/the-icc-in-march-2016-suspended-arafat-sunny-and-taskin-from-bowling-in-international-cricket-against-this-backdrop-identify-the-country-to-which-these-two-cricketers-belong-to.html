The ICC in March 2016 suspended Arafat Sunny and Taskin from bowling in international cricket. Against this backdrop identify the country to which these two cricketers belong to

(A) Pakistan

(B) South Africa

(C) ** Bangladesh

(D) West Indies

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bangladesh left-arm spinner Arafat Sunny and pacer Taskin Ahmed have been suspended from bowling in international cricket with immediate effect after an independent assessment found their bowling actions to be illegal.

Concept note-2: -The ICC has its headquarters in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. The ICC has 108 member nations currently: 12 Full Members that play Test matches, and 96 Associate Members.

Concept note-3: -There are 104 Member countries of the International Cricket Council (ICC). This includes 12 Full Members and 92 Associate Members.

Concept note-4: -This independent assessment found that some of Taskin’s standard deliveries exceeded the permissible level of elbow extension and, as such, his bowling action was found to be illegal and he was suspended from bowling in international cricket with immediate effect.

Concept note-5: -The International Cricket Council (ICC) is the global governing body for international cricket, encompassing men’s, women’s and youth participation and competition.
