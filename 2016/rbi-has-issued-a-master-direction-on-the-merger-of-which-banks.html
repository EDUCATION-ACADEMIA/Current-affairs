RBI has issued a master direction on the merger of which banks

(A) ** Private Sector Banks

(B) Foreign Banks

(C) Public Sector Banks

(D) Cooperative Banks

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Master Directions consolidate instructions on rules and regulations framed by the Reserve Bank under various Acts including banking issues and foreign exchange transactions.

Concept note-2: -During the bank mergers in India, the banks that were merged with the mammoth State Bank of India include the State Bank of Travancore, State Bank of Hyderabad, State Bank of Mysore, State Bank of Bikaner and Jaipur, State Bank of Patiala, and Bharatiya Manila Bank.

Concept note-3: -Currently in India there are 12 banks in number that are nationalised, and their names are Punjab National Bank, Bank of Baroda, Bank of India, Central Bank of India, Canara Bank, Union Bank of India, Indian Overseas Bank, Punjab, and Sind Bank, Indian Bank, UCO Bank, and Bank of Maharashtra, State Bank Of India.
