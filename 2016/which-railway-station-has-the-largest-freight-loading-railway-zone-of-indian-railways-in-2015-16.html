Which Railway Station has the largest freight loading railway zone of Indian Railways in 2015-16

(A) ** East Coast Railway

(B) Central Railway zone

(C) South East Central Railway

(D) South Central Railway

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The largest zone in Indian railways is the Northern Railway, whereas the smallest zone is the North-Eastern zone.

Concept note-2: -New Delhi Railway Station (NDLS) – 16 Platforms The railway station holds the record for being the largest railway station in India and the most complex interlocking system in the world. The New Delhi station handles more than 500, 000 passengers every day.

Concept note-3: -The Super Vasuki is operated by the South East Central Railway (SECR) zone of the Indian Railways. The SECR ran the record long-haul freight trains Vasuki and Trishul last year and the 2.8 km long SheshNaag train before that. The Super Vasuki was set up by amalgamating five rakes of goods trains as one unit.
