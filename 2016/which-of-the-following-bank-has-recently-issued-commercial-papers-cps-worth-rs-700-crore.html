Which of the following bank has recently issued commercial papers (CPs) worth Rs 700 crore.

(A) Citi Bank

(B) Yes bank

(C) DCB Bank

(D) ** IDFC Bank

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Guidelines for issue of CP are presently governed by various directives issued by the Reserve Bank of India, as amended from time to time. Commercial Paper (CP) is an unsecured money market instrument issued in the form of a promissory note.

Concept note-2: -The main issuers of commercial paper are finance companies and banks, but also include corporations with strong credit, and even foreign corporations and sovereign issuers. The main buyers of commercial paper are mutual funds, banks, insurance companies, and pension funds.

Concept note-3: -As you are aware, Commercial Paper (CP), an unsecured money market instrument issued in the form of a promissory note, was introduced in India in 1990 with a view to enabling highly rated corporate borrowers to diversify their sources of short-term borrowings and to provide an additional instrument to investors.

Concept note-4: -CP may be issued to and held by individuals, banking companies, other corporate bodies registered or incorporated in India and unincorporated bodies, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs).
