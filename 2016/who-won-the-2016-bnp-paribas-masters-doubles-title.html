Who won the 2016 BNP Paribas Masters Doubles title

(A) Fernando Verdasco and Bernard Tomic

(B) ** Henri Kontinen and John Peers

(C) Philipp Kohlschreiber and Ivo Karlovic

(D) Tommy Robredo and Ernest Gulbis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Two-time defending champion Novak Djokovic defeated Milos Raonic in the final, 6–2, 6–0 to win the men’s singles tennis title at the 2016 Indian Wells Masters. It was his record-equaling fifth Indian Wells title.

Concept note-2: -Only six players have won more than 10 Masters 1000 titles. With his victory at the 2021 Rolex Paris Masters, Novak Djokovic took sole possession of the ATP Masters 1000 titles record, breaking the record he previously shared with Rafael Nadal.

Concept note-3: -Djokovic won Bercy in 2009, 2013–15, 2019, 2021 and the French Open in 2016, 2021, Federer won the French Open in 2009 and Bercy in 2011 and Andre Agassi won Bercy in 1994 and both in 1999 as did Ilie Năstase in 1973. In 2015, Marcelo Melo and Ivan Dodig won the doubles events at both tournaments.
