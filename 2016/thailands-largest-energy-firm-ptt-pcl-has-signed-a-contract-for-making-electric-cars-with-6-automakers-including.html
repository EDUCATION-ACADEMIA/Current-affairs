Thailand’s largest energy firm PTT Pcl has signed a contract for making electric cars with 6 automakers including:

(A) BMW

(B) Mercedes Benz

(C) Nissan

(D) ** All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Thai government started its subsidy program for EVs from September 2022 to promote the use of EVs. The applicable EVs receive a subsidy of THB 70, 000-150, 000 per unit. The government also grants tax incentives for Evs including excise, road, and import tax reduction.

Concept note-2: -Domestic manufacturing and sales In 2021, 5, 781 battery-powered electric vehicles were registered in Thailand. These included 1, 955 cars, 3, 763 motorcycles, 32 tuk-tuks, 119 buses, and 2 trucks.

Concept note-3: -The latest incentive package announced includes: A 40 percent reduction in import duty for completely built-up (CBU) of battery EVs priced up to 2 million baht ($61, 805) and a 20 percent reduction for those priced between 2 million ($61, 805) and 7 million baht (US$211, 278) from 2022 to 2023; and.
