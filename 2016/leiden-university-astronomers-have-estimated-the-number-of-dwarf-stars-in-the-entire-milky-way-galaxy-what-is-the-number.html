Leiden University astronomers have estimated the number of dwarf stars in the entire Milky Way galaxy. What is the number

(A) 55 billion

(B) 60 billion

(C) ** 58 billion

(D) 62 billion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Two astronomy students from Leiden University have mapped the entire Milky Way Galaxy in dwarf stars for the first time. They show that there are a total of 58 billion dwarf stars, of which seven percent reside in the outer regions of our galaxy.

Concept note-2: -The Milky Way may contain ten billion white dwarfs, a billion neutron stars, and a hundred million stellar black holes. Filling the space between the stars is a disk of gas and dust called the interstellar medium.

Concept note-3: -Our galaxy, the Milky Way, is surrounded by about fifty dwarf galaxies. Most of these galaxies are only identifiable through telescopes and have been named after the constellation in which they appear on the sky (for example, Draco, Sculptor or Leo).

Concept note-4: -The Sun belongs to a galaxy called the Milky Way. Astronomers estimate there are about 100 thousand million stars in the Milky Way alone. Outside that, there are millions upon millions of other galaxies also!

Concept note-5: -Multiplying that by the Milky Way’s estimated 100 billion stars results in a large number indeed: 1, 000, 000, 000, 000, 000, 000, 000, 000 stars, or a “1” with 24 zeros after it (1 septillion in the American numbering system; 1 quadrillion in the European system).
