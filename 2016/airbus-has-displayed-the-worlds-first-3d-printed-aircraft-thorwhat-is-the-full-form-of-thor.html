Airbus has displayed the world’s first 3D-printed aircraft THOR.What is the full form of THOR

(A) ** Test of High-tech Objectives in Reality

(B) Tandy High-Performance Optical Recording

(C) Tactical Helicopter Offensive Response

(D) Tiered Hierarchy Overlayed Research

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -THOR stands for ‘Test of High-tech Objectives in Reality’ and resembles a large, white model airplane. Airbus sees the small pilotless propeller aircraft as “a test of what’s possible with 3D printing technology, ” said Detlev Konigorski, who was in charge of developing Thor, speaking at the air show.

Concept note-2: -Dwarfed by huge jets all around, the mini-plane Thor was nonetheless an eye-catcher at the Berlin air show-the small Airbus marvel is the world’s first 3D-printed aircraft.

Concept note-3: -When it comes to retrofitting aircraft, time is of the essence. But when newly designed elements in the cabin need to be integrated with existing components, spacer panels like those at the end of overhead compartments sometimes have to be adapted or redesigned.

Concept note-4: -Printable Aircraft The windowless, pilotless, and propeller-driven THOR weighs in at 21 kg, and measures less than 4 m long.

Concept note-5: -Airbus A300, the first aircraft launched by Airbus, introduced in 1974.
