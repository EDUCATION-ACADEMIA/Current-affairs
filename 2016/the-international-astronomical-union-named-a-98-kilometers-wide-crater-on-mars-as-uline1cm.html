The International Astronomical Union named a 9.8 kilometers wide crater on Mars as _________

(A) Tauthali

(B) ** Langtang

(C) Sangachok

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The International Astronomical Union has now named a 9.8km (six mile) wide crater on Mars as Langtang.

Concept note-2: -Jezero Crater sits within the Isidis Planitia region of Mars, where an ancient meteorite impact left behind a large crater some 750 miles (1, 200 kilometers) across. This event is known as Isidis impact, and it forever changed the rock at the base of the crater.

Concept note-3: -The largest impact basin on Mars is the Hellas Basin (also known as the Hellas Planitia), located in the southern hemisphere of the planet. This ancient impact crater has a diameter of 2, 299.16 km (1, 428 miles) and is 7.152 km (4.44 miles) deep (measured from Martian zero elevation).

Concept note-4: -When a meteor smacked into the surface of Mars with extremely high energy, pow! Not only did it punch an 11-mile-wide crater in the smoother terrain, it created a central peak in the middle of the crater.
