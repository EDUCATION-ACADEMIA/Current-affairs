Which country has launched the first satellite dubbed as The Tiantong-01 for mobile telecommunication.

(A) ** China

(B) US

(C) Russia

(D) Japan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tiantong (Chinese: ) is China’s first mobile communications satellite system. The first satellite Tiantong-1-01 was launched on August 6, 2016 (UTC+8).

Concept note-2: -It is the first satellite of China’s home-made satellite mobile telecom system, and a key part of the country’s space information infrastructure. More geostationary satellites will be sent into orbit for the system.

Concept note-3: -With the successful launch of Dong Fang Hong 1, China became the fifth country after the Soviet Union, United States, France, and Japan to independently launch a satellite.

Concept note-4: -On October 4, 1957, the Soviet Union launched the earth’s first artificial satellite, Sputnik I.

Concept note-5: -China 1, also called Chicom 1, or PRC 1, first Earth satellite orbited by the People’s Republic of China. It was launched on April 24, 1970, from the rocket facility at Shuang Cheng Tsu, and it made China the fifth nation to place a satellite into Earth orbit. Little is known about China 1.
