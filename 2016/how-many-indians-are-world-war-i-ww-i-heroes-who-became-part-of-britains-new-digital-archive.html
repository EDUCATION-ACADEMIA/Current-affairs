How many Indian’s are World War I (WW-I) heroes who became part of Britain’s new digital archive.

(A) 4

(B) 13

(C) ** 6

(D) 10

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Britain has launched a new digital archive telling the inspiring stories of war heroes, including six Indians, as part of the UK government’s centenary celebrations to mark World War I.

Concept note-2: -More than 1 million Indian soldiers served in World War I as part of the British India Army, and more than 74, 000 died in the conflict.

Concept note-3: -Some 1.5 million Indians served in the Indian Army during World War I and, of those, slightly more than one million deployed to battlefronts spanning Europe, Africa, and the Middle East. At the war’s end, India had suffered more than 120, 000 total casualties – men killed, wounded, or missing.

Concept note-4: -The Indian Army was involved in World War I via the British East India Company. Over one million Indian troops served overseas, of whom 62, 000 died and another 67, 000 were wounded. In total at least 74, 187 Indian soldiers died during the war.
