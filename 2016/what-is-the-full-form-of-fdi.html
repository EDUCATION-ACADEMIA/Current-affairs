What is the full form of FDI

(A) ** Foreign Direct Investment

(B) Financing Data Initiative

(C) Fault Detection and Isolation

(D) Finance Data Initiative

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) from the viewpoint of the Balance of Payments and the International Investment Position (IIP) share a same conceptual framework given by the International Monetary Fund (IMF).

Concept note-2: -A foreign direct investment refers to a purchase of a particular organisationâ€™s interest by another foreign organisation. Such an organisation or investor is located in a different country than the organisation whose interest is purchased.

Concept note-3: -Foreign direct investment (FDI) in India was introduced in the 1991 under the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA) implemented by the then finance minister, Dr. Manmohan Singh. It commenced with the baseline of 1 billion dollars in 1990.

Concept note-4: -Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) is the investment through capital instruments by a person resident outside India (a) in an unlisted Indian company; or (b) in 10 percent or more of the post issue paid-up equity capital on a fully diluted basis of a listed Indian company.
