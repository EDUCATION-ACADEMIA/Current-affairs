The Union Cabinet has given its approval to The High Courts (Alteration of Names) Bill, 2016 to changing the names of which two High Courts

(A) High Court of Calcutta, Bombay

(B) ** High Court of Madras, Bombay

(C) High Court of Calcutta, Madras

(D) High Court of Allahabad, Gauhati

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The High Courts (Alteration of Names) Bill, 2016 was introduced in Lok Sabha on July 19, 2016 by the Minister of Law and Justice, Mr. Ravi Shanker Prasad. The Bill seeks to modify the names of the High Courts (HCs)of Bombay, Calcutta and Madras.

Concept note-2: -Which are the High Courts in India that have Jurisdiction in more than two states? The High Courts of Mumbai and Guwahati have jurisdictions over more than two states. The Guwahati High Court extends its jurisdiction to states of Assam, Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, and Mizoram.

Concept note-3: -There are 25 High Courts in the country, three having jurisdiction over more than one State. Among the Union Territories Delhi, -and Union Territories of Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh have a High Court of their own.
