According to the National Sample Survey Office (NSSO), which state is the top rank in cleanest state in India

(A) ** Sikkim

(B) Kerala

(C) Himachal Pradesh

(D) Nagaland

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Sikkim. Sikkim has been declared as the cleanest state while Jharkhand comes last in the list. The results of the survey carried out last year by the National Sample Survey Office (NSSO) was released by Union Rural Development Minister Narendra Singh Tomar.

Concept note-2: -Retaining the “cleanest city” tag for the sixth time in a row, Indore took the top spot on the government’s annual cleanliness survey, while Madhya Pradesh retained the ‘cleanest state’ tag in the 7th edition of Swachh Survekshan Awards 2022, the results of which were announced Saturday.

Concept note-3: -10. Khargone, Madhya Pradesh. Apart from Indore, Khargone is yet another city from the state of Madhya Pradesh that made it to the list of the cleanest cities in India. By this, Madhya Pradesh is the cleanest state in India.

Concept note-4: -Sikkim and Kerala have been ranked as top-performing states in a Swachhta survey conducted by the National Sample Survey Office (NSSO). Swachh Survekshan (Cleanliness survey) is an annual survey of cleanliness, hygiene and sanitation in villages, cities and towns across India.

Concept note-5: -Recently, the President awarded Indore as the cleanest city for the sixth consecutive year as part of the Azadi75 Swachh Survekshan 2022, hosted as part of the Swachh Bharat Mission-Urban 2.0.
