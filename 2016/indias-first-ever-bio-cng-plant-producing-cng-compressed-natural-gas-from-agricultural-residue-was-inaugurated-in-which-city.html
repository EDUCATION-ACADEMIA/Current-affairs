India’s first ever BIO-CNG plant producing CNG (Compressed Natural Gas) from agricultural residue was inaugurated in which city

(A) ** Pune

(B) Visakhapatnam

(C) Jaipur

(D) Bhubaneshwar

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Recently, Indore emerged as the first municipality to get a bio-CNG plant, inaugurated by prime minister Narendra Modi in February this year.

Concept note-2: -The plant was installed on 15 acres of Indore Municipal Corporation trench land in the Devguradia area with an investment of Rs 150 crore through Public-Private Partnership (PPP) mode.

Concept note-3: -Public transport in Delhi was amended by the Supreme Court of India to use Compressed Natural Gas (CNG) instead of diesel or petrol. After the implementation of CNG since April 2001, Delhi has the highest fraction of CNG-run public vehicles in the world and most of them were introduced within 20 months.

Concept note-4: -Primove has set up first 2G BioCNG plant in India at Pirangut in Pune which was inaugurated in August 2016 at the hands of Hon. Shri. Nitin Gadkari, Minister of Road, Transport and Highways and Hon. Shri.

Concept note-5: -India’s first BIO-CNG fuel plant was inaugurated in Pune, Maharashtra, by Union Minister of Surface Transport Nitin Gadkari and former Union Defence Minister Manohar Parrikar.
