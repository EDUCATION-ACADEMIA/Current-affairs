A new Guinness World Record for the largest Lego sculpture has been set in which of the following country

(A) USA

(B) Japan

(C) China

(D) ** UK

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Video: Guinness World Record for largest Lego model set in UK.

Concept note-2: -Largest LEGO® Sculpture (number of bricks) This huge 13-metre high LEGO® version of London’s Tower Bridge created for Land Rover to launch their New Discovery car is nothing short of impressive and record breaking. Made using a total of 5, 805, 846 individual pieces, it beat the previous record by 470, 646 bricks.

Concept note-3: -Cooper, who is 13, recently took on his biggest LEGO challenge yet, completing the LEGO World Map for a Guinness World Record. It has 11, 695 pieces and isn’t a typical, 3-D model but instead a flat mosaic spread. The time to beat was 12 hours. Cooper completed the map in 9 hours, 14 minutes and 49 seconds.

Concept note-4: -LEGOLAND Germany welcomed more than 2, 800 AFOL guests to set a new world record for the largest LEGO city ever constructed. Using more than 1.8 million bricks, eight AFOL clubs from around Germany banded together to build 1, 556 buildings and roughly 1, 000 road elements.
