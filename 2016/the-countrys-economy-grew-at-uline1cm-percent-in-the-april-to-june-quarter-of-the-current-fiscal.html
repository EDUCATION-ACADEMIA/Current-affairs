The country’s economy grew at _________ Percent in the April to June quarter of the current fiscal.

(A) ** 7.1

(B) 7.6

(C) 7.4

(D) 7.3

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -India’s GDP grew at 13.5 per cent in the April-June quarter of the financial year 2022-23, according to the data released by the NSO.

Concept note-2: -5. GDP at Constant (2011-12) Prices in April-September 2022-23 (H1 2022-23) is estimated at ₹75.02 lakh crore as against ₹68.36 lakh crores during the corresponding period of previous year, showing a growth of 9.7 percent in H1 2022-23 as against 13.7 percent during the same period last year.

Concept note-3: -India’s GDP grows at 13.5% in April-June quarter.

Concept note-4: -The last time India’s GDP achieved higher annual growth was in April-June 2021, when it was 20.1% higher than the pandemic-depressed level of a year before. Forecasts for the latest quarter ranged from 9.0% to 21.5%.

Concept note-5: -"Real GDP or GDP at Constant (2011-12) Prices in Q2 2022-23 is estimated at Rs 38.17 lakh crore, as against Rs 35.89 lakh crore in Q2 2021-22, showing a growth of 6.3 percent as compared to 8.4 percent in Q2 2021-22, ” stated govt’s press release on GPD numbers.
