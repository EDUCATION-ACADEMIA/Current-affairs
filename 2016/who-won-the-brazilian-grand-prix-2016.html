Who won the Brazilian Grand Prix 2016

(A) Romain Grosjean

(B) Nico Rosberg

(C) Max Verstappen

(D) ** Lewis Hamilton

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hamilton took pole position and won the race from Rosberg to take the championship to the final round in Abu Dhabi. Max Verstappen finished third, having dropped to 16th after a pit stop with 16 laps to go.

Concept note-2: -Lewis Hamilton won a rain-soaked 2016 Brazilian Formula One Grand Prix after leading from start to finish on the Interlagos track at Sao Paulo on Sunday. The 31-year-old finished ahead of Mercedes team-mate Nico Rosberg, meaning he’s pushed the fight for the world championship to the season’s final race at Abu Dhabi.

Concept note-3: -Hamilton, meanwhile, said on the radio that it was “no racing incident.” The stewards handed Verstappen a five-second penalty for the collision, but many fans were left undecided over who was to blame.

Concept note-4: -Max Verstappen has been fined €50, 000 for touching and examining Lewis Hamilton’s rear wing in Parc Ferme after qualifying in Brazil – but has not been given a grid penalty of any kind for the offence.

Concept note-5: -George Russell won the Brazilian Grand Prix at Interlagos on Sunday, the 21st round of the Formula 1 world championship, leading home a Mercedes 1-2 for his maiden F1 victory.
