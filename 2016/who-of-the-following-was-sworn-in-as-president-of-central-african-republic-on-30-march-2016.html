Who of the following was sworn-in as President of Central African Republic on 30 March 2016

(A) Catherine Samba-Panza

(B) Alexandre-Ferdinand Nguendet

(C) Michel Djotodia

(D) ** Faustin Archange Touadera

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The current President of the Central African Republic is Faustin-Archange Touadéra, since 30 March 2016.

Concept note-2: -David Dacko (French pronunciation: [david dako]; 1927 – 21 November 2003) was a Central African politician who served as the first president of the Central African Republic from 14 August 1960 to 1 January 1966, and 3rd President from 21 September 1979 to 1 September 1981.

Concept note-3: -The Central African Republic is roughly the size of France and is bordered by Chad to the north, Sudan and South Sudan to the north and east, the Democratic Republic of the Congo (Kinshasa) and the Republic of the Congo (Brazzaville) to the south, and Cameroon to the west.

Concept note-4: -Faustin-Archange Touadéra (French: [fostɛ̃ aʁkɑ̃ʒ twadeʁa]; born 21 April 1957) is a Central African politician and academic who has been President of the Central African Republic since March 2016. He previously was Prime Minister of the Central African Republic from January 2008 to January 2013.

Concept note-5: -Central Africa, defined by the UN Subregion, consists of the following countries: Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, Congo Republic-Brazzaville, Democratic Republic of Congo, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, São Tomé & Principe.
