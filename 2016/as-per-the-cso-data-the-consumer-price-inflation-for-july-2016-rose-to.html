As per the CSO data, the Consumer Price Inflation for July 2016 rose to

(A) 0.76 %

(B) 0.98 %

(C) 0.54 %

(D) ** 6.07 %

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Headline CPI inflation declined to 3.4 per cent in 2018-19 from 3.6 per cent in 2017-18, 4.5 per cent in 2016-17, 4.9 per cent in 2015-16 and 5.9 per cent in 2014-15.

Concept note-2: -The All-India CPI-IW for September, 2022 increased by 1.1 points and stood at 131.3 (one hundred thirty-one point three). On 1-month percentage change, it increased by 0.84 per cent with respect to previous month compared to an increase of 0.24 per cent recorded between corresponding months a year ago.

Concept note-3: -The dollar had an average inflation rate of 2.13% per year between 2016 and 2017, producing a cumulative price increase of 2.13%.

Concept note-4: -The correct answer is IV Only. Consumer Price Index for Urban Non-Manual Employees is compiled by the Central Statistical Organization (CSO).
