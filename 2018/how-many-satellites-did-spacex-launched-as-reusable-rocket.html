How many satellites did SpaceX launched as Reusable Rocket?

(A) ** 64

(B) 57

(C) 75

(D) 83

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The 56 Starlink satellites, weighing a combined 17.4 metric tonnes (19.4 tons), according to a SpaceX commentator, were protected by a five times reused fairing during the ascent. The rocket lifted off smoothly from the Cape Canaveral Space Force Station in Florida after a ten-minute delay at 4:32 a.m. EST (0922 GMT).

Concept note-2: -A SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket launched into a brilliant blue sky this morning (Jan. 3) from Cape Canaveral Space Force Station in Florida, carrying 114 satellites to orbit-the second-most spacecraft ever lofted on a single mission.

Concept note-3: -Out of the rockets recovered it has reused Falcon 9 first-stage boosters 87 times.

Concept note-4: -FCC authorizes SpaceX Gen2 Starlink: Up to 7, 500 satellites.

Concept note-5: -As of November 2022, there are 3, 271 Starlink satellites in orbit, of which 3, 236 are operational, according to Astronomer Jonathan McDowell who tracks the constellation on his website (opens in new tab).
