Which country launches twin BeiDou-3 navigation satellites into space?

(A) ** China

(B) Pakistan

(C) Saudi Arabia

(D) Denmark

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 2015, China launched the third generation BeiDou system (BeiDou-3) for global coverage.

Concept note-2: -China on November 4, 2022 outlined plans to further expand the global reach of its home-grown Beidou satellite navigation system, billed as its alternative to America’s Global Positioning System (GPS).

Concept note-3: -China successfully launched the final satellite of its Beidou navigation system into space on Tuesday.

Concept note-4: -China’s BeiDou navigation project was launched in the early 1990s. The system then became operational within China in 2000 and in the Asia-Pacific region in 2012.

Concept note-5: -Jielong-3 has close similarities in terms of lift capacity, length and diameter (2.65 meters), payload fairing (3.35 meters) and mass at liftoff with the ZK-1A rocket developed and recently launched by a group under the Chinese Academy of Sciences.
