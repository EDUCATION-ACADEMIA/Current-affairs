What dispute is holding back Russia and Japan from signing the peace treaty which was proposed by the Russian President?

(A) Trade dispute

(B) Financial dispute

(C) Partnership dispute

(D) ** Territorial dispute

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Kuril Islands dispute, known as the Northern Territories dispute in Japan, is a territorial dispute between Japan and Russia over the ownership of the four southernmost Kuril Islands.

Concept note-2: -The Treaty of Portsmouth formally ended the Russo-Japanese War of 1904–05. The negotiations took place in August in Portsmouth, New Hampshire, and were brokered in part by U.S. President Theodore Roosevelt.

Concept note-3: -Russo-Japanese War, (1904–05) Conflict between Russia and Japan over territorial expansion in East Asia. After Russia leased the strategically important Port Arthur (now Lüshun, China) and expanded into Manchuria (northeastern China), it faced the increasing power of Japan.

Concept note-4: -The war happened because the Russian Empire and Japanese Empire disagreed over who should get parts of Manchuria and Korea.

Concept note-5: -The Soviet Union did not sign the Treaty of Peace with Japan in 1951. On October 19, 1956, Japan and the Soviet Union signed a Joint Declaration providing for the end of the state of war and for the restoration of diplomatic relations between both countries.
