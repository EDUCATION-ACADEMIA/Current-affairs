ISRO to launch its heaviest satellite so far - GSAT-11 on November 30. GSAT-11 weighing over

(A) 6, 300-kg

(B) ** 5, 700-kg

(C) 4, 800-kg

(D) 7, 200-kg

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The 5854-kg GSAT-11 will provide high data rate connectivity to users of Indian mainland and islands through 32 user beams in Ku-band and 8 hub beams in Ka-band.

Concept note-2: -Weighing 3136 kg at lift-off, GSAT-19 is the heaviest satellite launched from the Indian soil.

Concept note-3: -India’s next generation high throughput communication satellite, GSAT-11 was successfully launched on December 05, 2018 from Kourou launch base, French Guiana by Ariane-5 VA-246. Weighing about 5854 kg, GSAT-11 is the heaviest satellite built by ISRO.

Concept note-4: -Recently, PSLV-C54 was successfully launched EOS-06 satellite on 26th November 2022 along with Eight Nano-satellites including INDIA-BHUTAN SAT (INS-2B).

Concept note-5: -ISRO’s work horse PSLV-C54 has successfully launched EOS-06 satellite along with Eight Nano-satellites into two different SSPOs. The mission was accomplished from Satish Dhawan Space Centre SHAR on 26th November 2022.
