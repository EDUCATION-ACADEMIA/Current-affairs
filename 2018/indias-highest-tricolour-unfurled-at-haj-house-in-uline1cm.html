India’s Highest Tricolour Unfurled At Haj House in _________?

(A) ** Mumbai

(B) Chennai

(C) New Delhi

(D) Hyderabad

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bhikaji Cama was one of the pioneers of India’s struggle for independence. On August 21, 1907, an International Socialist Conference was being held in Stuttgart, a city in Germany. On this occasion, Bhikaji Rustam Cama unfurled the first version of the Indian National Flag.

Concept note-2: -It was unfurled by the first Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru at the Princess Park near India Gate in New Delhi on August 15, 1947.

Concept note-3: -The tallest flag pole for flying India’s national flag is in Belagavi, Karnataka - 361 ft. The 2nd tallest is in Attari-Wagha border - 360 ft. The 3rd tallest is in Ranchi, Jharkhand - 293 ft.

Concept note-4: -Presently, Belagavi or Belgaum Fort in Kote Kere, Karnataka, has the tallest Indian flagpoles measuring 361 ft, just one foot taller than the flag at the Attari border.
