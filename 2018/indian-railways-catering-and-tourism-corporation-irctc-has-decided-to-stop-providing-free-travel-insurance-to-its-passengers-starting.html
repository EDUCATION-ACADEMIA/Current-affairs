Indian Railways Catering and Tourism Corporation (IRCTC) has decided to stop providing ‘free travel insurance’ to its passengers starting

(A) October 2, 2018

(B) ** September 1, 2018

(C) August 30, 2018

(D) November 14, 2018

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It offers a coverage of up to Rs. 10 lakhs and covers events such as train accidents leading to death or permanent disability of the passenger and even robbery, loot, and other hazards. If you do purchase this travel insurance, your journey from the point of origin to the destination will be covered for only 49 paise.

Concept note-2: -The present tagline is “Lifeline of the nation”. The IRCTC Logo depicts pan India connectivity of railway networks.

Concept note-3: -Get only once chance. Indian Railways says that the transfer of tickets can be done only once, that is, if the passenger has transferred his ticket once to another person, then he cannot change it, that is, now this ticket cannot be transferred to anyone else.

Concept note-4: -The Bharat Gaurav trains were launched by the Railways Ministry to be operated in places having touristic importance and promote theme-based tourism.
