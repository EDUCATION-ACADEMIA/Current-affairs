What is the expected weight of Satellites to be put in space with the upgraded high-thrust Vikas engine?

(A) 3200 kg

(B) 3500 kg

(C) ** 4000 kg

(D) 4300 kg

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The main beneficiary of the high-thrust Vikas engine is said to be the heavy-lifting GSLV-Mark III launcher, which ISRO expects will now put 4, 000-kg satellites to space.

Concept note-2: -The overall length of the vehicle is 43.5 m with a gross lift-off weight of 640 tonnes and a 5m-diameter payload fairing. . GSLV Mk-III (LVM3) is the new heavy lift launch vehicle of ISRO for achieving a 4000 kg spacecraft launching capability to GTO (Geosynchronous Transfer Orbit) in a cost effective manner.

Concept note-3: -Vehicle Specifications It can take up to 1, 750 kg of payload to Sun-Synchronous Polar Orbits of 600 km altitude.

Concept note-4: -Addressing the seventh ‘Bengaluru Space Expo 2022’ and later talking to reporters, he noted that at present it takes about USD 10, 000 to USD 15, 000 to put a one-kg payload into orbit. “We have to bring it down to USD 5, 000 or even USD 1, 000 per kg.
