In which campus did Bombay Natural History Society (BNHS) start its regional center?

(A) ** Wetland Research and Training Center

(B) Institute Of Environmental Studies And Wetland Management

(C) Indian Institute of Science Education and Research

(D) Indian Institute of Technology

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Bombay Natural History Society (BNHS) opened its regional centre on the campus of Wetland Research and Training Centre near Chilika Lake in Odisha. About: It is a Non-Governmental Organisation in India engaged in conservation and biodiversity research.

Concept note-2: -The Bombay Natural History Society was established in 1883. It is one of the oldest non-governmental organisation of its kind in India.

Concept note-3: -The Bombay Natural History Society is one of the largest non-governmental organisations in India engaged in conservation and biodiversity research. It was founded in 15 September 1883, For more relevant articles refer to the links given below: UPSC Environment and Ecology Questions.

Concept note-4: -The Bombay Natural History Society (BNHS), a pan-India wildlife research organization, has been promoting the cause of nature conservation since 1883.

Concept note-5: -BNHS is one of the oldest scientific organizations in India, working for nature conservation since 1883. Our primary goal is to spread awareness about nature through science-based research, conservation advocacy, education, scientific publications, nature tours, and other programs.
