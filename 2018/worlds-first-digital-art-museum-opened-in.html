World’s first digital art museum opened in

(A) Russia’s Moscow

(B) Germany’s Berlin

(C) ** Japan’s Tokyo

(D) China’s Beijing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Japanese art collective teamLab has opened a bricks and mortar museum packed with 50 of its technicolour digital artworks in Tokyo’s Odaiba.

Concept note-2: -In 1734, almost 60 years before the Louvre made its debut in Paris, the Museo Capitolino (Capitoline Museum) opened in Rome. Established under Pope Clement XII, it was the first public art museum of international importance and served as the model for such institutions as we know them today.

Concept note-3: -Be touched” the Digital Art Museum is a monumental art space for digitally created art that is intended to touch visitors and be touched by them. The museum is dedicated to engage audiences with multidimensional and multisensual immersive art experiences which works without the usual distance between art and viewer.

Concept note-4: -The first use of the term digital art was in the early 1980s when computer engineers devised a paint program which was used by the pioneering digital artist Harold Cohen. This became known as AARON, a robotic machine designed to make large drawings on sheets of paper placed on the floor.
