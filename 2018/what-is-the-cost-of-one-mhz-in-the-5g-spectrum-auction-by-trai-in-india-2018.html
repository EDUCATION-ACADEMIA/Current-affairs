What is the cost of one MHz in the 5G spectrum auction by TRAI in India, 2018?

(A) ** Rs.492 crore per MHz

(B) Rs.6, 500 crore per MHz

(C) Rs.875 crore per MHz

(D) Rs.235 crore per MHz

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In its recommendations for the auction of the 5G spectrum, the telecom regulatory body suggested that the base price for prime 5G band of 3300-3670 MHz be kept at Rs 317 crore per MHz, which is 35 per cent lower than the base price of Rs 492 crore per MHz announced in 2018.

Concept note-2: -The price of the spectrum increased by more than 44.8 per cent over the base price of Rs 18.65 crore per block (each block of 0.2 MHz).

Concept note-3: -On April 11, the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (Trai) had recommended a reserve price of Rs 317 crore a unit for airwaves in the premium 3.3-3.67 GHz 5G band (also known as C-band), a 36% reduction to its last recommendation in 2018.

Concept note-4: -In the 2022 5G Spectrum auction, Reliance Jio emerged as the top buyer in the auction of airwaves, spending over Rs 88, 000 crore, more than half the total bidding amount of Rs 1.5 trillion.

Concept note-5: -As per the new proposals, the base price for the coveted 700 MHz band works out to be ₹3, 927 crore per MHz, which is 40 per cent lower than earlier.
