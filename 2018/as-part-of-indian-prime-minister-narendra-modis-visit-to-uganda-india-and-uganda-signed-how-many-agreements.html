As part of Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s visit to Uganda, India and Uganda signed how many agreements?

(A) Six

(B) Three

(C) Five

(D) ** Four

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Major commodities of imports from Uganda to India are edible vegetables and certain roots and tubers, coffee, tea, mate and spices, wood and articles of wood, wood charcoal, cotton, essential oils, and cocoa and cocoa preparation.

Concept note-2: -There is a sizable community of people of Indian origin living in Uganda. In 2003, there were an estimated 15,000 people of Asian descent (majority of Indians and Pakistanis) living in Uganda, compared to approximately 80,000 before they were expelled by the dictator Idi Amin in 1972.

Concept note-3: -Following the proclamation of the State of Uganda on 9 October 1963, the Kabaka (King) of Buganda, Edward Mutesa II, became the first President of Uganda. Uganda did not become a republic de jure until 1966 with Obote’s conflict with President Edward Mutesa II.

Concept note-4: -Apollo Milton Obote (28 December 1925 – 10 October 2005) was a Ugandan political leader who led Uganda to independence from British colonial rule in 1962.
