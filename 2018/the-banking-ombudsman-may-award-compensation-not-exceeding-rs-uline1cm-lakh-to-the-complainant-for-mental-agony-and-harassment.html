The Banking Ombudsman may award compensation not exceeding Rs _________ lakh to the complainant for mental agony and harassment.

(A) ** One

(B) Two

(C) Three

(D) Four

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Can compensation be claimed for mental agony and harassment? The Banking Ombudsman may award compensation not exceeding ₹ 1 lakh (₹ One Hundred Thousand) to the complainant for mental agony and harassment.

Concept note-2: -However, for any consequential loss suffered by the complainant, the Ombudsman shall have the power to provide a compensation up to Rupees 20 lakh, in addition to, up to Rupees One lakh for the loss of the complainant’s time, expenses incurred and for harassment/mental anguish suffered by the complainant.

Concept note-3: -Yes. The Banking Ombudsman may reject a complaint at any stage if it appears to him that a complaint made to him is: not on the grounds of complaint referred to above. compensation sought from the Banking Ombudsman is beyond ₹ 20 lakh (₹ Two Million).

Concept note-4: -(9) The bank shall, unless it has preferred an appeal under Sub-Clause (1) of Clause 14, within one month from the date of receipt by it of the acceptance in writing of the Award by the complainant under Sub-Clause (8), comply with the Award and intimate compliance to the Banking Ombudsman.
