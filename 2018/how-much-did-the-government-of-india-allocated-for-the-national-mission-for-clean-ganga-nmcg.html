How much did the government of India allocated for the National Mission for Clean Ganga (NMCG)?

(A) Rs.1, 000 crore

(B) Rs.10, 000 crore

(C) Rs.2, 000 crore

(D) ** Rs.20, 000 crore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It was inaugurated in June 2014, with a budget of Rs. 20, 000 (US$ 2.7 billion) to achieve two objectives, i.e., conservation and rejuvenation of Ganga.

Concept note-2: -The Union government spent more than Rs 13, 000 crore on cleaning the Ganga since 2014, with Uttar Pradesh receiving the largest outlay among states, the National Mission for Clean Ganga (NMCG) is learnt to have informed the National Ganga Council on Friday.

Concept note-3: -NMCG. ‘Namami Gange Programme’, is an Integrated Conservation Mission, approved as ‘Flagship Programme’ by the Union Government in June 2014 with budget outlay of Rs.20, 000 Crore to accomplish the twin objectives of effective abatement of pollution, conservation and rejuvenation of National River Ganga.

Concept note-4: -The National Mission for Clean Ganga (NMCG) was implemented by the National Council for Rejuvenation, Protection and Management of River Ganga also known as the National Ganga Council. This mission was established in 12th August 2011 under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 as a registered society.
