Who is the author of the famous book Bend in the River book?

(A) Vikram Seth

(B) ** Naipaul

(C) R.K.Narayanan

(D) Jhumpa Lahiri

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A Bend in the River is a 1979 novel by Nobel laureate V. S. Naipaul. The novel, telling the story of Salim, a merchant in post-colonial mid-20th century Africa, is one of Naipaul’s best known works and was widely praised. It was shortlisted for the Booker Prize in 1979.

Concept note-2: -About the Author V.S. Naipaul was born in Trinidad in 1932.

Concept note-3: -The narrator, Salim, a Muslim Indian merchant, opens a store in a sleepy small town at a bend in the river (ostensibly the Congo River).

Concept note-4: -In the “brilliant novel” (The New York Times) V.S. Naipaul takes us deeply into the life of one man-an Indian who, uprooted by the bloody tides of Third World history, has come to live in an isolated town at the bend of a great river in a newly independent African nation.

Concept note-5: -Elisha Cooper’s spectacular new picture book River is a journey story and so much more.
