Who won Singapore Grand Prix 2018?

(A) ** Lewis Hamilton

(B) Sebastian Vettel

(C) Kyle Busch

(D) Denny Hamlin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lewis Hamilton won the race to further strengthen his lead in the Championship after Vettel finished 3rd, thus losing further ground to Hamilton, Max Verstappen managed 2nd place.

Concept note-2: -Lewis Hamilton won the race to further strengthen his lead in the Championship after Vettel finished 3rd, thus losing further ground to Hamilton, Max Verstappen managed 2nd place.

Concept note-3: -Sergio Perez of Red Bull was reprimanded by race stewards after the Singapore Grand Prix and received a five seconds penalty for an incident on Lap 36. But the Mexican driver retained the win as he had finished seven seconds faster than second-placed Charles Leclerc of Ferrari.

Concept note-4: -Despite a five-second time penalty, the race was won by Red Bull Racing driver Sergio Pérez, with pole sitter Charles Leclerc finishing second and his Ferrari teammate Carlos Sainz Jr.

Concept note-5: -Vettel’s largest margin of victory in his career was at the 2013 Singapore Grand Prix, a race where he finished 32.627 seconds ahead of the second-placed Ferrari of Fernando Alonso, and the smallest margin of victory was at the 2011 Spanish Grand Prix, where he beat McLaren’s Lewis Hamilton by 0.630 seconds.
