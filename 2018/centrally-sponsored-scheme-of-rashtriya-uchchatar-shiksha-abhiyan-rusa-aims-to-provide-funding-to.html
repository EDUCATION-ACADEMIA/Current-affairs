Centrally Sponsored Scheme of Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA) aims to provide Funding to

(A) ** Higher educational Institutions in India

(B) AIIMS Hospitals in India

(C) Banks in India

(D) Public Sector Companies in India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Rashtriya Uchchatar Shiksha Abhiyan (RUSA), a Centrally Sponsored Scheme (CSS) is an overarching scheme, operating in mission mode for funding the state government universities and colleges to achieve the aims of equity, access and excellence.

Concept note-2: -To upgrade high potential existing colleges into Universities or Model Degree Colleges. To identify and fill critical infrastructural gaps in higher education. To ensure adequate availability of quality faculty. Facilitate research & innovation in the State /higher Education institutions.

Concept note-3: -Funding process RUSA is provided by the central Ministry of Human Resource Development directly to the state and UT governments. From the state/UT budget the funds are disbursed to individual institutions. The funding to states would be made on the basis of critical appraisal of state plans for higher education plans.

Concept note-4: -RUSA is a Centrally Sponsored Scheme (C.S.S.), launched in 2013 aims at providing strategic funding to eligible state Higher Educational Institutions. The Central Funding (in the ratio of 65:35 for General Category States and 90:10 for Special Category States) would be norm based and outcome dependent.

Concept note-5: -The central funding is norm-based and outcome-dependent (60:40 for general category states, 90:10 for special category states, and 100 percent for union territories).
