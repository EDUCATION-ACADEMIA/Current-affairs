Which country became the first team in FIFA World Cup history to use fourth substitute?

(A) Germany

(B) Portugal

(C) ** Russia

(D) Spain

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Brazil, with five wins, are the only team to have played in every tournament. The other World Cup winners are Germany and Italy, with four titles each; Argentina, with three titles; France and inaugural winner Uruguay, each with two titles; and England and Spain, with one title each.

Concept note-2: -The four stars above Uruguay’s football crest represent the four FIFA recognized senior (non-amateur) world titles won by the Uruguay national football team in 1924, 1928, 1930 and 1950.

Concept note-3: -The first group was the only one to contain four teams: Argentina, Chile, France, and Mexico.

Concept note-4: -The first FIFA World Cup™ was one of a kind. Taking place wholly in the Uruguayan capital of Montevideo, the sport’s inaugural showpiece was rich in details that might bemuse the modern football fan: four teams arriving together on the same boat, an unfinished stadium, even a one-armed goalscorer in the Final.
