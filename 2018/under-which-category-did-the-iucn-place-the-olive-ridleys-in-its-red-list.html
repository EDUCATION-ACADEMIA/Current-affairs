Under which category did the IUCN place the Olive Ridleys in its Red list?

(A) Endangered

(B) ** Vulnerable

(C) Critically endangered

(D) Least concern

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Olive ridley turtles are recognized as Vulnerable by the IUCN Red list. 2. International Trade in these turtles and their products is banned under CITES Appendix I.

Concept note-2: -The species is listed as Vulnerable in the IUCN Red List, Appendix 1 in CITES, and Schedule 1 in Wildlife Protection Act, 1972.

Concept note-3: -It is listed as endangered in IUCN Red List and has been included in the Schedule I of the Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972.

Concept note-4: -Population Status Olive ridley turtles are found worldwide and listed under the Endangered Species Act. Their breeding colony populations on the Pacific Coast of Mexico are listed as endangered; all others are listed as threatened.

Concept note-5: -Certain populations of olive ridleys are federally listed as threatened and endangered, and the sea turtle is internationally listed as vulnerable. Threats include harvesting for skin and meat, accidental capture (particularly in shrimp trawl nets and nearshore gill nets), and marine pollution.
