Great Britain, U.S, and France met in October to decide if ____ violated a peace keeping treaty by selling kamikaze drones to Russia.

(A) China

(B) Ukraine

(C) Japan

(D) ** Iran

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -One senior military official said that partnership had deepened quickly, after Iran’s agreement to supply drones to the Russians last summer “bailed Putin out.”

Concept note-2: -A Ukrainian senior intelligence official says Russia has received a new shipment of Iranian-made Shahed-136 kamikaze drones from the Islamic Republic of Iran.

Concept note-3: -In May 2022, Tajikistan opened a factory to produce Iran’s Ababil-2 reconnaissance and attack drones. Around 2010, Venezuela started assembling Mohajer-2 surveillance drones. By 2021, Iran and its allies were assembling drones in Syria.

Concept note-4: -Two factors help explain Iran’s export of drones to Russia: Moscow’s need for a cheap, expendable unmanned system to target Ukrainian infrastructure, and Iran’s strategic interest in upending the U.S.-led world order and enhancing its geopolitical influence.
