Workers’ rights

(A) We all have the right to a good life. Mothers and children, people who are old, unemployed or disabled, and all people have the right to be cared for

(B) ** Every grown-up has the right to do a job, to a fair wage for their work, and to join a trade union.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Trade unions are entitled to negotiate with employers on term of employment without hindrance. The freedom of a trade union to negotiate with employers to try and conclude collective agreements is protected.

Concept note-2: -Workers’ rights encompass a large array of human rights from the right to decent work and freedom of association to equal opportunity and protection against discrimination. Specific rights related to the workplace include health and safety in the workplace and the right to privacy at work, amongst many others.

Concept note-3: -In Manitoba, legislation dictates four key rights that all workers have: the right to know, the right to participate, the right to refuse, and the right to protection against reprisal. Workers have the right to know about workplace safety and health hazards.

Concept note-4: -Everyone who works has the right to just and favourable remuneration ensuring for himself and his family an existence worthy of human dignity, and supplemented, if necessary, by other means of social protection. Everyone has the right to form and to join trade unions for the protection of his interests.
