The 3rd unit of Kakrapar atomic power project has become India’s 1st 700 MW unit to achieve criticality. Where does the Kakrapar atomic power plant located?

(A) Maharashtra

(B) ** Gujarat

(C) Punjab

(D) Kerala

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Kakrapar Atomic Power Station is a nuclear power station in India, which lies in the proximity of Mandvi, Surat and Tapi river in the state of Gujarat.

Concept note-2: -About Kakrapar Nuclear Power Plant The Canadian company, Candu Energy, supplied the Pressurised Heavy Water Reactors (PHWR) used in the plant. The first commercial nuclear power station in India is operated by India’s Nuclear Power Corporation.

Concept note-3: -Significance of Achievement: KAPP-3 is the country’s first 700 MWe (megawatt electric) unit, and the biggest indigenously developed variant of the Pressurised Heavy Water Reactor (PHWR). The PHWRs uses natural uranium as fuel and heavy water as moderator. They are the mainstay of India’s nuclear reactor fleet.

Concept note-4: -Nuclear power stations in India are located in Kaiga in Karnataka, Kalpakkam in Tamilnadu, Tarapur in Maharashtra, Kakrapara in Gujarat, Rawat Bhata in Rajasthan and Naraura in Uttar Pradesh.

Concept note-5: -Unit 3 of the Kakrapar Atomic Power Project (KAPP) is expected to commence commercial operation by December 2022 after obtaining stage-wise regulatory clearances. Unit-3 (KAPP-3) is India’s first 700 MWe (megawatt electric) Pressurized Heavy Water Reactor (PHWR) with innovative features.
