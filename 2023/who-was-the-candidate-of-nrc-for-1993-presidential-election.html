Who was the candidate of NRC for 1993 presidential election?

(A) Shehu Shagari

(B) Obafemi Awolowo

(C) ** Bashir Tofa

(D) MKO Abiola

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bashir Othman Tofa (20 June 1947 – 3 January 2022) was a Nigerian politician. A Kanuri Muslim who hailed from Kano State, Tofa was the National Republican Convention (NRC) candidate in the annulled Nigeria’s 12 June 1993 presidential election, which was organised by the military government of General Ibrahim Babangida .

Concept note-2: -At the end of the election, MKO Abiola was said to have won over eight million votes winning in 19 states while his opponent, the NRC candidate Tofa received over six million votes and winning in 10 states.

Concept note-3: -The final vote was leaked on 18 June by democracy activists defying the law, revealing Abiola won by a 58% majority.

Concept note-4: -Sylvester Ugoh was the Vice Presidential candidate of the National Republican Convention in 1993, the NRC’s Presidential candidate being Bashir Tofa.
