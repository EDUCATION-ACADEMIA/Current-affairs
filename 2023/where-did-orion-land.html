Where did Orion land?

(A) Orion landed in the Atlantic Ocean.

(B) Orion landed in the Indian Ocean.

(C) Orion landed in the Pacific Ocean.

(D) Orion landed in the Southern Ocean.

(E) ** Orion landed in the Pacific Ocean.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -NASA’s Orion spacecraft splashed down in the Pacific Ocean, west of Baja California, at 9:40 a.m. PST Sunday after a record-breaking mission, traveling more than 1.4 million miles on a path around the Moon and returning safely to Earth, completing the Artemis I flight test.

Concept note-2: -Sign up for The Tech Friend newsletter. The Orion spacecraft, which had no astronauts on board, splashed down in the Pacific Ocean off the Baja California peninsula of Mexico under a trio of billowing parachutes at 12:40 p.m. Eastern time.

Concept note-3: -As the Orion spacecraft plummets through Earth’s atmosphere, its three main parachutes have deployed to slow the capsule to a splashdown in the Pacific Ocean.

Concept note-4: -NASA’s Orion Spacecraft Successfully Returns to Earth After Historic Moon Mission. NASA’s Orion space capsule splashed down safely in the Pacific on Sunday, completing the Artemis 1 mission – a more than 25-day journey around the Moon with an eye to returning humans there in just a few years.
