When is the World Rare Disease Day observed across the world?

(A) 28 February

(B) ** 29 February

(C) 27 February

(D) 26 February

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Rare Disease Day is celebrated every year on the last day of the February month. The first Rare Disease Day was celebrated in 2008 on 29 February, a ‘rare’ date that happens only once every four years.

Concept note-2: -The 100-day countdown for the World Rare Disease Day-2023 celebration, which falls on February 28, has started with the right earnestness.

Concept note-3: -Rare Disease Day is observed every year on 28 February (or 29 in leap years)-the rarest day of the year.

Concept note-4: -World Cancer Day: World Cancer Day is celebrated every year on February 4. It is observed to unite the world in the fight against cancer. World Cancer Day aims to save millions of lives each year through education, raising awareness and pressing governments and individuals worldwide to take action.

Concept note-5: -On March 4, 2022, FDA joined the global observance of Rare Disease Day, which was created to raise awareness about the 7, 000 known rare diseases, many of which have no treatment.
