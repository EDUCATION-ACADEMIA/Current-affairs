What is the target for agricultural and processed food products from India for 2022-23?

(A) USD 3.56 billion

(B) USD 13.56 billion

(C) USD 43.56 billion

(D) ** USD 23.56 billion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As per the DGCI&S provisional data, for the year 2022-23, an export target of USD 23.56 billion has been fixed for the agricultural and processed food products basket and an export of USD 9.59 billion has already been achieved in the first four months of the current fiscal.

Concept note-2: -During April-December 2022-23, overall exports rose 9% to $332.76 billion while imports increased 24.96% to %551.7 billion. Trade deficit during the nine-month period widened to $218.94 billion as against $136.45 billion in April-December 2021-22.

Concept note-3: -Exports of Agricultural Products in India averaged 100.46 INR Billion from 1991 until 2022, reaching an all time high of 386.50 INR Billion in December of 2022 and a record low of 4.95 INR Billion in October of 1991. This page includes a chart with historical data for India Exports of Agricultural Products.

Concept note-4: -Agri & allied products exports were valued at US$ 37.3 billion, recording a growth of 17% over 2020-21. Rice is the largest exported agricultural product from India and contributed to more than 19% of the total agriculture export during the year 2021-22.

Concept note-5: -50 percent of U.S. agricultural imports are horticultural products: fruits, vegetables, tree nuts, wine, essential oils, nursery stock, cut flowers, and hops.
