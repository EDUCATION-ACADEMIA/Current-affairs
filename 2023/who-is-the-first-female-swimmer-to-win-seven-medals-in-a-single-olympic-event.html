Who is the first female swimmer to win seven medals in a single Olympic event?

(A) Janet Evans

(B) Katie Ledecky

(C) ** Emma Mckeon

(D) Inge de Brujin

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Emma McKeon is the first female swimmer to win seven medals at Single Olympic games, she is a citizen of which of the following countries ?

Concept note-2: -McKeon won seven medals in Tokyo, the most by any female swimmer at a single Olympic Games, and equalled the most medals won by a female athlete in any sport at a single Olympic Games, tying Soviet gymnast Maria Gorokhovskaya.

Concept note-3: -On this day in 1972, Mark Spitz became the first man to win seven Olympic Gold medals at a single Olympic Games.

Concept note-4: -Emma McKeon, Kyle Chalmers win gold on a brilliant night for Aussies at world swimming championships. Emma McKeon and Kyle Chalmers have kept the gold rush coming for Australia, claiming their maiden individual world titles at the world short course swimming championships.
