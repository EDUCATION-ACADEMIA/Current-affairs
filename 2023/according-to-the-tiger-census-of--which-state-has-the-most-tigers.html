According to the tiger census of , which state has the most tigers?

(A) Karnataka

(B) ** Madhya Pradesh

(C) Maharashtra

(D) Kerala

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Madhya Pradesh regained the top position in the 2018 tiger census after it was found to be home to 526 felines, two more than Karnataka (524).

Concept note-2: -Madhya Pradesh has the highest population of Tigers in India, with 526 Tigers being preserved in the six tiger reserves in their natural habitat.

Concept note-3: -Bandhavgarh National Park has the highest density of tigers in India. Entirely there are six tiger reserves in India. In fact, two of its tiger reserves are known to spot the wild cat-Kanha and Bandhavgarh National Park.

Concept note-4: -Madhya pradesh has the highest number of tigers(526) in the age group of 1.5 years with more than 408 big cats. Other states with significant populations include Karnataka (524), Uttarakhand (442), Tamil Nadu (229), Maharashtra (190), Assam (167), Kerala (136) and Uttar Pradesh (117).

Concept note-5: -Ans. The state with the highest number of tigers is Madhya Pradesh at about 526 as per the latest counting. It is also the state with the highest number of tiger reserves.
