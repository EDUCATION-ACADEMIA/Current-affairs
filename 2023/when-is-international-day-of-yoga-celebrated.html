When is International Day of Yoga celebrated?

(A) ** 21 June

(B) 22 June

(C) 23 June

(D) 24 June

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When proposing 21 June as the date, Modi said that the date was the longest day of the year in the northern hemisphere (shortest in the southern hemisphere), having special significance in many parts of the world. In Indian calendars, the summer solstice marks the transition to Dakshinayana.

Concept note-2: -THE ORIGIN OF INTERNATIONAL DAY OF YOGA (IDY) Yoga is a 5, 000-year-old tradition from India that combines physical, mental and spiritual pursuits to achieve harmony of the body and mind. On 11th December 2014, the United Nations General Assembly declared June 21st as the International Day of Yoga.

Concept note-3: -International Yoga Day 2022: It is observed on June 21 to spread awareness about the benefits of yoga. This year it is the 8th edition of the International Yoga Day. The main event of the International Yoga Day 2022 demonstration will be held at Mysuru, Karnataka.

Concept note-4: -The first International Day of Yoga was observed around the world on June 21, 2015. PM Modi, during his speech at the UNGA, said, “Yoga is an invaluable gift of India’s ancient tradition.

Concept note-5: -World Music Day is celebrated on 21 June to promote music on an international level and is a way to establish global harmony through music.
