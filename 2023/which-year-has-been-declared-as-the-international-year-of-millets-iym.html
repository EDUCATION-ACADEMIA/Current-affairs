Which year has been declared as the ‘International Year of Millets (IYM)’?

(A) [A] 2021

(B) [B] 2022

(C) ** [C] 2023

(D) D] 2024

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Watch | Business Matters | Why did UN declare 2023 as International Year of Millets?-The Hindu.

Concept note-2: -Once a staple in traditional Indian cooking, millets fell out of favour over the years, and have been making a slow comeback in India and across the world. To keep this momentum going, the United Nations has declared 2023 the International Year of Millets.

Concept note-3: -India had also sent a proposal to the UN for declaring 2023 as year of millets. This proposal was supported by 72 countries and the Food and Agricultural Organization and UNGA declared 2023 as International Year of Millets in March 2021.

Concept note-4: -India’s proposal to observe an International Year of Millets in 2023 was approved by the Food and Agriculture Organisation (FAO) in 2018 and the United Nations General Assembly has declared the year 2023 as the International Year of Millets.

Concept note-5: -The UN has designated 2024 as the International Year of Camelids. Camels, llamas, alpacas, vicuñas and guanacos are an important source of livelihood for millions of families-most of them pastoralists-in dryland and mountainous rangeland ecosystems around the world.
