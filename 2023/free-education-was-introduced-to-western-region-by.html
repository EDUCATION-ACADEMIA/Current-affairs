Free education was introduced to western region by

(A) James Ibori

(B) ** Obafemi Awolowo

(C) Sir Fedrick Lordlugard

(D) Herbert Macluay

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -However, in 1955, Dayo’s fortune changed when the premier of the Western Region, Chief Obafemi Awolowo introduced free education to the region. Dayo enrolled and spent years in school without having to worry about fees.

Concept note-2: -It is no longer news that late sage, Chief Obafemi Awolowo introduced free education in the 50s in the old Western region. But what many don’t know is the brain behind the policy. He is Professor Stephen Awokoya.

Concept note-3: -In 1955, the government of Western Region of Nigeria introduced a free primary education programme. That scheme, which featured prominently up till 1966, suffered a major blow, following the military take-over of government in that year.

Concept note-4: -However, the General Olusegun Obasanjo led federal government launched the universal free primary education programme on September 6th 1976, the first time the programme was launched Page 2 nation wide.

Concept note-5: -In January 1955, the Western Region started a free universal primary education scheme. In the words of Chief Awokoya, the bill that brought this into being was “the greatest piece of social legislation that has ever been made in the country” [7].
