Name the Anti-tank guided Nag missile which was tested by DRDO at interim test range in balasore

(A) ** Dhruvastra

(B) Agni

(C) Brahmos

(D) Prahaar

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Indian armed forces successfully test fired the helicopter-launched Nag Missile (HELINA), now named Dhruvastra anti-tank guided missile in direct and top attack mode that is upgraded with new features.

Concept note-2: -Range : 500m – Min. and 7000m (Max.)

Concept note-3: -Understanding ATGM Indigenously-developed Laser-Guided Anti-Tank Guided Missile (ATGM) employs a tandem High Explosive Anti-Tank (HEAT) warhead to defeat Explosive Reactive Armour (ERA) protected armoured vehicles.

Concept note-4: -The Nag missile is a Indian third-generation, all-weather, fire-and-forget, lock-on after launch, top-attack, anti-tank guided missile with an operational range of 500 metres to 7000 metres km with the potential of being developed in future versions to 20, 000 metres.

Concept note-5: -It is an all-weather condition with day and night capabilities and with a minimum range of 500m and a maximum range of 4 km.
