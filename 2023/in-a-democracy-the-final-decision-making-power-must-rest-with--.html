In a democracy the final decision making power must rest with ____ ?

(A) The official heads

(B) The monarchs

(C) ** Those elected by the people

(D) None

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Who gives the government this power to make decisions and enforce laws? The answer to this question depends on the type of government there is in a country. In a democracy it is the people who give the government this power. They do this through elections in which they vote for particular persons and elect them.

Concept note-2: -The Prime Minister of India is the leader of the ruling party in the Lok Sabha. From the MPs who belong to her party, the Prime Minister selects ministers to work with her to implement decisions.

Concept note-3: -Such an agreement is an outcome of deliberation, i.e. a process where people try to persuade each other through the give-and-take of rational arguments. Thus in a democratic system, the decision making is delayed because of this deliberations and negotiations.

Concept note-4: -A democratic decision always involves many persons, discussions and meetings. When a number of people put their heads together, they are able to point out possible mistakes in any decision. As most of the decisions are taken by discussions, this reduces the chances of rash or irresponsible decisions.
