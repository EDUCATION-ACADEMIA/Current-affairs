What is happening in the Gulf States that is of major concern?

(A) ** A large hurricane is making landfall

(B) An Oil Spill

(C) An Earthquake

(D) Monsters

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When hurricanes make landfall, they begin to decrease in strength because they no longer have the ocean water from which to gain energy. However, they are still dangerous and can cause much damage.

Concept note-2: -They produce strong winds, storm surge flooding, and heavy rainfall that can lead to inland flooding, tornadoes, and rip currents.

Concept note-3: -What is Storm Surge? Storm surge is caused primarily by the strong winds in a hurricane or tropical storm. The low pressure of the storm has minimal contribution! The wind circulation around the eye of a hurricane (left above) blows on the ocean surface and produces a vertical circulation in the ocean (right above).

Concept note-4: -Hurricanes form over the warm ocean water of the tropics. When warm moist air over the water rises, it is replaced by cooler air. The cooler air will then warm and start to rise. This cycle causes huge storm clouds to form.
