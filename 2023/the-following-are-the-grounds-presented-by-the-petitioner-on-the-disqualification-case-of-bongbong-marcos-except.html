The following are the grounds presented by the petitioner on the disqualification case of Bongbong Marcos, except;

(A) Failure to file income tax returns from 1982 to 1985

(B) The election code disqualifies a person who has been sentenced to more than 18 month of prison

(C) ** Marcos on Monday called these petitions “nuisance.”

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The petitions were denied by the Commission on Elections and the Supreme Court. Marcos won the election in a landslide victory, garnering 58.77% of the vote with a margin of 30.83% over the second placer, Leni Robredo.

Concept note-2: -Estimates of the amount the Marcoses reportedly acquired in the last few years of the Marcos administration range from US$5 billion to $13 billion. No exact figures can be determined for the amount acquired through the entire 21 years of the Marcos regime.

Concept note-3: -This is a list of major acts and legislation which were signed by Philippine President Bongbong Marcos, including landmark bills which lapsed into law during his presidency. He has signed four laws in the 19th Congress, three of which are national in scope.
