IMF funds come from 2 major sources namely ____

(A) Quotas & Grants

(B) Grants & Loans

(C) Quotas from member/ non-member countries

(D) ** Quotas & loans

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -IMF funds come from three sources: member quotas, multilateral and bilateral borrowing agreements. Member quotas are the primary source of IMF funding. A member country’s quota reflects its size and position in the world economy. Read more on how the IMF regularly reviews quotas.

Concept note-2: -What are IMF quotas? Quotas are the building blocks of the IMF’s financial and governance structure. An individual member country’s quota broadly reflects its relative position in the world economy. Quotas are denominated in Special Drawing Rights (SDRs), the IMF’s unit of account.

Concept note-3: -Main Functions The IMF employs three main functions – surveillance, financial assistance, and technical assistance – to promote the stability of the international monetary and financial system.
